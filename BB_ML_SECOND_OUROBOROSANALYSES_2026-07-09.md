## BB useful findings

### 1. Anywhere possible versus campaign curation

Thesis: BB should allow rainbow magic dimensional travel from one text, media object, book, screen, mirror, image, song, or archive to another.

Antithesis: if everything becomes a portal, BB loses curation and becomes vibes.

Synthesis: any threshold can be portal substrate, but only some thresholds become campaign dimensions. The gate is teaching value, internal logic, age fit, citation discipline, quest motive, and consequence capacity.

Filed to bb*: dim-034.

### 2. Mirror magic versus backend leakage

Thesis: the mirror screenshot is good BB. Reflection difference naturally becomes shadow-realm portal logic.

Antithesis: saying portal-class, substrate, overlay, layer, or dimension architecture at the table kills the magic.

Synthesis: front-facing table language says mirror, page, screen, book, picture, song, or door. Backend terms stay in BB and CC files.

Filed to bb*: dim-034 and cross-referenced to dm-095.

### 3. Assignments as quests versus homework beside game

Thesis: assignments should be quests.

Antithesis: a worksheet with fantasy labels remains homework beside the game.

Synthesis: an assignment becomes a quest only when the academic work has an in-world reason and a future consequence.

Existing bb* already carried the assignments-as-quests rule. The new pass hardens future consequence.

Filed to bb*: dm-111.

### 4. Timebound consequences versus punishment trap

Thesis: unfinished, weak, or strong work should have future consequences like Kingdom Come Deliverance.

Antithesis: classroom consequences can become punitive, unfair, or identity-marking.

Synthesis: consequences are recoverable traces. Bad work produces scars, lost time, weaker leverage, repair quests, trust rebuilding, second attempts, or later echoes. The student is never frozen into failure.

Filed to bb*: dm-111.

### 5. Kingdom Come realism versus teacher workload

Thesis: KCD-style time systems are excellent for consequence and world-realism.

Antithesis: every NPC routine and every time window can overload the teacher.

Synthesis: use tiered time design. Named NPCs get schedules. Background people get routine templates. Quest windows get explicit open, close, success, weak-success, failure, repair, and later echo.

Filed to bb*: dm-111.

### 6. Cross-media travel versus flattening

Thesis: the bookworm can move between books, media, images, games, and archives.

Antithesis: cross-media travel can flatten each medium into generic content.

Synthesis: the bookworm travels. The medium stays itself. Macbeth remains Macbeth. Hughes remains Hughes. The Reddit mirror joke remains a Reddit joke. BB names the crossing operation and tracks the learning trace.

Filed to bb*: bw-012.

### 7. Canon seal versus player agency

Thesis: source worlds need a sealed exegetical main.

Antithesis: sealed canon can make player choices feel decorative.

Synthesis: canon spine stays sealed, but surrounding scenes, routes, evidence access, NPC trust, timing, repair paths, and bookworm development remain live.

No new entry needed. Existing bb*/cc* doctrine already carries this. The new timebound rule strengthens the variable-surroundings side.
