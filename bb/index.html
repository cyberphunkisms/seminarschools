bookwormburrows·enter the burrow

BB is an AI-assisted reading and seminar game where a human Dimensional Master and AI work together, texts become worlds, wormcards become characters, study becomes adventure, and player work changes what the world remembers.

How BB is played

  1. Choose the anchor text. A story, poem, article, speech, myth, play, film, image, song, game, or historical document becomes the world.
  2. Make wormcards. Each player builds a burrowing character who can step through pages, mirrors, screens, images, and other thresholds.
  3. Give the wormcards to the Dimensional Master. The AI translates each bookworm into an in-game character, while the human Dimensional Master mediates the table and protects the source.
  4. Open the burrow. Play happens around, before, after, beside, and through the world the text or medium makes possible.
  5. Turn assignments into quests. A letter, journal, debate, research note, paragraph, map, scene, or reflection gives the character something to gain, repair, unlock, discover, or risk.
  6. Let work quality drive consequence. There are no dice. Careful reading, strong writing, research, revision, and discussion decide what the world can return.
  7. Let time matter. Quest windows can open, close, echo later, or become repair paths depending on what the player finishes and how well it is done.
  8. Record the adventure as learning. The group leaves with written work, quests completed, scars, discoveries, open problems, and stronger reasons to keep studying.

Made first for voluntary seminar play

BB is built for reading groups, clubs, tutoring, families, adult seminars, independent learners, and other groups that choose to enter together. Schools can use the same world logic: required work becomes quests, roles, discoveries, and consequences inside the game.
