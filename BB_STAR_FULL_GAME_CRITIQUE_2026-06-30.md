# BB* / PolymythDND full game critique — 2026-06-30

## Working definition

BookwormBurrows, BB, and PolymythDND name the same working game in different registers.

BB is a teacher-led, AI-assisted, diceless academic TTRPG where texts become worlds, wormcards become character sheets, assignments become quests, and the Dimensional Master uses AI behind the curtain to track continuity while preserving narrative, pedagogy, and sealed exegesis.

## Strongest parts of the game

### 1. Assignments as quests is the core engine

The strongest idea in BB is that academic work becomes the action system. A normal assignment asks a student to complete schoolwork because school requires it. BB gives the assignment a diegetic reason. The student writes, researches, maps, debates, revises, or reflects because the character needs to act in the world.

This is the difference between gamified homework and BB. Gamified homework adds points to schoolwork. BB makes the schoolwork the way the game moves.

### 2. Sealed exegesis plus living surround solves the central text-game problem

A classroom game around a text can easily become random fan fiction. BB avoids that by sealing the core text. The story’s actual sequence and meaning stay intact. Students play in the world around the text, before it, after it, beside it, through its social conditions, and through assignments that extrapolate from it.

The sealed center protects reading. The living surround creates play.

### 3. The teacher-AI split is now clearer

The teacher is the Dimensional Master. AI is the table assistant. AI tracks the bureaucracy: time, locations, NPC schedules, inventories, quest ledgers, consequences, and continuity. The teacher remains the filter, the voice, the pedagogue, and the social authority.

This prevents the main gorgonification risk: AI becoming the DM instead of supporting the DM.

### 4. Burrowing animals make the identity system coherent

The burrowing animal is not decorative. It matches the ontology of the game. The player enters texts through burrows, holes, tunnels, and wormholes. The bookworm is a reader who eats and moves through text. The wormcard is a character sheet shaped by that passage.

### 5. No dice keeps academic quality primary

Dice would pull the game toward random success. BB’s stronger principle is that reading, writing, research, discussion, revision, and interpretive care decide what happens. This makes academic quality the engine of consequence.

### 6. Adult seminar mode expands the project without flattening it

BB can work for children, teens, university students, PhD students, and adult reading groups. The adult mode keeps the same grammar: wormcards first, human Dimensional Master, AI behind the curtain, assignments as quests, independent study motivated by in-game need.

## Main weaknesses and risks

### 1. Assignment-to-quest integration needs to be made table-ready

The biggest remaining weakness is practical. The principle is clear: assignments are quests. The table-ready conversion still needs templates.

Each assignment should specify:

- the academic deliverable
- the in-game motive
- the character or group reason to care
- the setupnpcs connected to the task
- the real learning attached to those setupnpcs
- the possible in-game consequence
- the teacher judgment criteria
- the way the sealed exegesis remains protected

Thank You M’am should be audited first because it is already the clearest seed.

### 2. The NPC taxonomy was under-specified until now

BB needs two NPC types.

Setupnpcs are pregame arena NPCs. They should usually be real people, documented figures, institutional actors, or historically grounded roles tied to assignments, real-life events, and real learning.

Improvnpcs are live-play ordinary people. They populate the world during play, such as neighbors, shopkeepers, bystanders, guards, clerks, classmates, drivers, or passersby.

This taxonomy must be carried into campaigncodex templates and DM assistant prompts.

### 3. The 221-entry BB archive is too mixed for live use

The archive currently contains methodology, design ledger, rules, teacher notes, AI conduct doctrine, pending questions, student-friendly explanations, and campaign-building logic in one large library.

That is useful as a source archive. It is heavy for a teacher running a session.

The next step is not deletion. The next step is layering:

- teacher manual
- DM quickstart
- AI table-assistant doctrine
- player-facing explanation
- mechanics reference
- campaign-building guide
- unresolved design ledger

### 4. The no-dice system needs a simple teacher-facing outcome rubric

No dice is correct. The teacher still needs a fast way to decide what happens.

A simple live rubric could use:

- reading care
- assignment quality
- voice or role-fit
- relevance to the text-world
- revision or preparation
- collaboration

The result can be full success, partial success, complication, delay, social cost, clue gained, route unlocked, relationship changed, or quest advanced.

### 5. Exegesis protection needs examples of valid and invalid extrapolation

The principle is strong. Teachers need examples.

Valid extrapolation:

- Roger writes to Mrs. Jones twenty years later.
- Students research Harlem in 1958.
- Students map where Roger may have run.
- Students write Mrs. Jones’s private reflection without contradicting the story.
- Students follow the consequences after the story ends.

Invalid extrapolation:

- Changing what happens inside Hughes’s original scene.
- Turning Mrs. Jones into a random combat NPC.
- Making the story’s moral meaning depend on a gimmick.
- Inventing a twist that overrides the text’s restraint.

### 6. Adult mode needs independent-study quest forms

For university students, PhD students, and adult seminars, the game should motivate self-study. That means quests should ask players to bring external research back into the world.

Adult quest examples:

- document trail quest
- theory encounter quest
- historical context quest
- source comparison quest
- archival reconstruction quest
- seminar defense quest
- counter-reading quest
- translation and reception quest

### 7. The website can still accidentally smell like the game itself

The website supports the game. The game happens through the Dimensional Master, the wormcards, the text, the assignments, and the table.

Future UI should avoid chatbot-game framing. The better tool is a DM table assistant that starts by asking for wormcards and the anchor text.

### 8. The teacher role needs firm protection in every future tool

AI is always prone to gorgonification. Every future BB tool should preserve teacher-facing output by default. Student-facing AI output should only exist when the teacher deliberately opens that channel.
