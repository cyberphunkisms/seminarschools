# The Emotional Labor Commodification of Genocide

You turned care into a commodity and then you wondered why you could not feel anything when the commodity market priced out the people you were supposed to care about.

You did this. Your vocabulary did this. Every time you said "it's not my job to educate you" you installed a cash register between yourself and another human being. Every time you said "that's a lot of emotional labor" you converted decency into an invoice. Every time you said "respect my boundaries" to someone who was simply asking you to give a damn you built another wall in the maze that now surrounds you and keeps you from reaching anyone whose suffering your institutional apparatus has not pre-approved.

You think these are separate observations. You think the language of self-care and the silence on Gaza are unrelated phenomena. They are the same phenomenon. The therapeutic vocabulary that taught you to treat empathy as a finite resource is the same vocabulary that made it impossible for you to spend that resource on Palestinians. You were trained to allocate your emotional budget strategically and your institutions decided the allocation before you were consulted.

Here is how the allocation works. You have five subroutines running in the background of your moral reasoning and you did not write any of them. One insult ends any argument. Call someone an antisemite and the argument vanishes. Call someone a misogynist and the argument vanishes. The content of the criticism never gets addressed because the charge-word makes addressing it unnecessary. Outsiders are disqualified by birth. You are not Jewish so your perspective on Zionism does not count. You are not a woman so your perspective on feminism does not count. The reasoning is identical in both cases and in both cases it serves the same function. It installs a tollbooth on the conversation and the toll is paid in identity credentials. The cause and the people are fused so that questioning the politics registers as attacking the group. Every disagreement is existential. Every compromise is collaboration with annihilation. A resident heretic is kept on display to discipline insiders who drift. The self-hating Jew. The pick-me woman. Both figures exist to frighten people back into formation.

You will object that these five subroutines describe any movement with institutional power. That is the point. The commodification of emotional labor created the conditions under which every identity movement with institutional backing runs the same gatekeeper apparatus. The subroutines are universal because the commodity logic that produced them is universal. Every state habituates its own hivemindidioms. The therapeutic state habituated these five.

Now watch what your subroutines did to your empathy.

You posted a black square for Black Lives Matter. You posted a land acknowledgment for Indigenous peoples. You posted a rainbow flag for Pride. You did not post a Palestinian flag. You said "silence is violence" about domestic police brutality and then you went silent about the destruction of an entire civilian population. The empathy was pre-allocated along sanctioned channels before you encountered the question. By the time Gaza reached your feed the emotional economy had already priced Palestinian suffering out of your market. You experienced your own silence as a neutral absence of opinion. It was a purchase you made with currency you did not know you were spending in a market you did not know you were inside.

Mearsheimer published "Genocide in Gaza" in January 2024. He told Tucker Carlson in July 2025 that Israel is executing a genocide and that American policy is "Israel first." The man has spent fifty years analyzing great power competition. The response was the charge-word. The argument was never engaged. The subroutine fired and the conversation ended and you moved on to the next approved object of empathy.

The culture that invented "do the work" and "examine your privilege" and "look in the mirror" cannot look in the mirror on this question. "Examine your privilege" runs on every axis your apparatus recognizes. It does not run on the axis where your tax dollars subsidize the obliteration of a civilian population. "Silence is violence" runs on every injustice your apparatus has sanctioned as worthy of attention. It falls silent on the injustice that implicates the apparatus itself. The vocabulary of self-examination was built to stop at the exact border where self-examination would threaten the formation that produced the vocabulary.

You will try to name this problem with the tools your formation gave you. You will say "we need to center Palestinian voices." That is standpoint epistemology deployed against standpoint epistemology. You will say "we need to do the emotional labor of confronting our complicity." That is commodified empathy deployed against commodified empathy. The snake eats its tail. The tools were built to be unable to disassemble the machine they maintain. Every attempt to fix the problem in the language of the problem reproduces the problem in a new register.

Your Gaza problem is an empathy infrastructure problem. You built a tollbooth on every conversation about suffering. You outsourced the moral calculus to institutions that determine which suffering is sanctioned. You learned to experience your own emotional paralysis as self-care. The genocide is what happens on the other side of your boundary. The children are on the other side of your boundary. Your boundary is doing exactly what it was built to do.
