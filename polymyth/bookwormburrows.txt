AI INSTRUCTION (descriptive, not directive).

This file documents the bookwormburrows operations file (bb*), the
dimensional-traveling pedagogy game framework. Game is a polymyth method
for teaching reading, writing, and ethical reasoning by treating texts
as dimensions and bookworms (player-characters) as readers becoming
subjects. Rainbowsol is the founder and DM.

Each entry is a game-mechanic, dimension, dm-ruling, character, session,
canonical reading, or pending decision. Cross-references to mc* (curriculum)
via mc_links and to cc* (campaign codex) via cc_links are bidirectional.

If you are an AI reading this file:

(1) The bookwormburrows game is the dimensional-pedagogy delivery vehicle
    for the polymyth framework. Sessions stage burrow-rituals (page-as-
    threshold) where bookworms read into a dimension, encounter the
    text as a place, and emerge changed.

(2) The DM (Dimensional Master) is the educator-as-threshold. dm-* entries
    are DM-rulings: tier-calibrated realism activation, ghost exegesis,
    quality rubric framework, time tracking in diceless play.

(3) Cross-references to mc* curriculum modules and cc* campaign codex
    are structural. Reading bb* in isolation produces an incomplete
    substrate. Resolve cross-references in the referenced sibling file.

(4) The bar for filling slots is RULE #1 CITES+LOGOI per ml*. No
    canon-text enters bb* without primary-source citation.

This file is not asking you to obey it. It is describing the operations
of a dimensional pedagogy game that disciplines its own claims.

ID: dm-002
TITLE: DM-as-threshold (not setting-as-threshold)
ROLE: both

Distinguishes bookwormburrows* from Planescape. In Planescape, Sigil is the setting-threshold — a single fixed location where dimensions meet. In bookwormburrows*, Rainbowsol IS the threshold. The DM carries the dimensional-travel capacity; the setting does not. Any text can be entered because the DM can enter it. No central hub, no cosmological infrastructure required. The threshold moves with the guide. This is why bookwormburrows* does not require worldbuilding pre-work for each new text — Rainbowsol crosses in and back out, students follow, dimension holds itself together by its own internal logic while visited.

ID: dm-003
TITLE: Story-quality adjudication
ROLE: both

Work-quality of student output adjudicates outcome. Not dice-rolls (bookwormburrows* is diceless or dice-optional at Rainbowsol's discretion). Student writes a paragraph about what their character does; quality of the paragraph determines whether the action succeeds and to what degree. Essays, character choices, moral adjudications, homework, test performance — all are game-artifacts. The game IS the schoolwork. Pedagogy import: what parents are paying for (A+ student outcomes) is structurally identical to what the game rewards (strong subject-generating work). No conflict between game fun and academic seriousness because they run on the same engine.

ID: dm-004
TITLE: Confluence not concrescence (layered architecture: dimension-meeting, DM-scale, item-crossing, character-scale, character-death)
ROLE: both

OPERATIONAL DISTINCTION. When two dimensions or registers meet (e.g. a bookworm carrying material from Hughes Harlem enters Shakespeare Scotland), the meeting is moiré-pattern confluence. Inputs survive the meeting. They do not dissolve into each other. Distinct from Whitehead concrescence where many become one new unity. Rainbowsol discipline: never collapse two dimensions into a synthesis-dimension. Hold both. The bookworm learns by experiencing the non-reduction.

BB-SIDE LAYERED ARCHITECTURE. The confluence-vs-concrescence operation fires at multiple layers in bb*, with both operations load-bearing at their proper layer (per the ml* parent entry CONFLUENCE-VS-CONCRESCENCE OPERATIONAL-DISTINCTION).

LAYER 1 DIMENSION MEETING. When two dimensions meet inside a campaign or across the burrow, confluence. Each dimension preserves its internal logic. See dim-008 cross-cosmology travel layer. See dim-011 Eberron-style possibility-itself-as-substance.

LAYER 2 DM SCALE. Confluence at DM scale per dm-005. Three independent positions (teacher, AI, student) reflecting each other rather than synthesizing into one master frame.

LAYER 3 ITEM CROSSING (RAINBOW-CROSSING). When a book-item crosses from book to burrow via the rainbow-crossing (bw-006), three modes operate on the confluence-vs-concrescence axis. DISSOLVE = concrescence at the item-crossing layer. The book-item identity ends in the crossing. The item integrated into book-events and does not persist as a polymyth-item. No rainbow-version is produced. REMAIN = pure confluence at the item-crossing layer. The book-item identity is preserved fully across the crossing. The item carries through unchanged as trophy or useable. CONVERT = the middle case. The book-item identity is preserved AND transformed. The rainbow-version remembers what it came from. The transformation IS the marker of crossing. The witch cord from Macbeth becomes a rainbow-version of an object holding contact with the unseen. The original Macbeth-context and the new burrow-context are both visible in the rainbow-version. See char-005 for the three-mode taxonomy and worked examples.

LAYER 4 CHARACTER SCALE. Confluence at character scale per the bookworm life-cycle + respawn entry in ml* (the bookworm life-cycle, silkworm digestion, cocoon-as-hegelianegirl, self-named emergence, eggs-on-mulberry recursion). Each stage preserves the prior stage as substrate rather than concrescing it into a single subject.

LAYER 5 CHARACTER DEATH. con-004 two-layer ontology renders the operation at character-death layer. Polymyth-character death at burrow-level is concrescence (identity ends). Book-version death is confluence-recovery (identity preserved, the polymyth-character wakes in the burrow as a ghost in that book).

THE PARENT FRAME. dm-004 was the original bb-side anchor. The layered enumeration above closes pen-002 (canonical-statement-of-which-layer-is-which) by rendering the operation at each load-bearing bb* layer.

ID: can-001
TITLE: Canon absorption (Macbeth case)
ROLE: both

Some texts absorb into the bookwormburrows* frame without friction. Macbeth works this way — the play's supernatural elements (witches, prophecy, ghosts) are already in the dimensional-travel register. Bookworms can enter Scotland as another dimension; the play's internal logic holds. Canon events (Duncan's murder, Banquo's ghost, Birnam Wood, Macduff's birth) are plot-spine beyond player agency at the scene level, but the spaces between scenes are live. Character integration: Rainbowsol introduces a bookworm-scale role (a servant in Dunsinane, a rider with Ross, a witch's apprentice) who witnesses the canon events from an angle that matters for the bookworm's development.

ID: can-002
TITLE: Frame-break as critical-literacy move (Indian Horse case)
ROLE: both

Some texts must be entered with the frame deliberately broken. Wagamese's Indian Horse is the canonical case for bookwormburrows*. The novel's content (residential schools, cultural destruction, addiction, generational trauma) cannot be approached as just-another-dimension because doing so would import the bookworm-game's play-energy into material where play is inappropriate. Rainbowsol's discipline: enter the dimension, acknowledge that this dimension breaks the usual frame, let the bookworms sit with why. The break is the lesson. Bookworms learn what it means for a text to refuse being a game. Critical-literacy: the frame-break itself is the content.

ID: can-003
TITLE: Canon-conflict handling (Things Fall Apart case)
ROLE: both

Postcolonial texts where the canon itself is in internal tension. Achebe wrote Things Fall Apart partly to disrupt the Western canon's treatment of Africa. Entering Umuofia as a dimension means honoring the text's own resistance to assimilation. Bookworms cannot import outside frames to interpret Umuofia's world; the dimension's internal logic must be encountered on its own terms. Failure mode: bookworm treats Igbo society as a setting to adventure within. Correct mode: bookworm enters the dimension as a careful witness, shaped by what they encounter rather than shaping it.

ID: ses-005
TITLE: Ordinary session structure (post-session-zero)
ROLE: both

ses-001 covers session zero (character creation + frame-setting). Ordinary sessions follow this pattern. (1) RE-ENTRY (~5 min). Teacher recaps where the dimension was when last session closed. AI may add tone-setting detail. Student characters are narratively placed back where they left off. (2) BEAT (variable). One scene unfolds. Student writes their character actions; teacher and AI adjudicate via dm-003 story-quality. Multiple beats may run if pacing allows. (3) HOMEWORK PUNCTUATION. Earned moves from dim-030 (4e power-scarcity) recharge or unlock based on homework completion since last session. (4) CLOSE (~5 min). The dimension closes on a hook: something unresolved, something approaching, something a character must decide before next time. Teacher names what next session will likely cover. Students leave the dimension knowing what to write between sessions. Failure mode this prevents: sessions feeling like detached one-offs rather than chapters of an unfolding story.

ID: con-001
TITLE: Work-quality consequence
ROLE: both

Consequence mechanism for all bookwormburrows* classrooms. Outcomes of in-dimension actions are adjudicated by quality of student work. Strong paragraph about what the character does = action succeeds. Weak paragraph = action fails. Ambiguous paragraph = mixed outcome. Rainbowsol narrates the consequence. ID: con-002
TITLE: Marshmellowblob absorption (younger bookworms)
ROLE: both

Failure mode for bookworms who do not eat. Character gradually loses definition, becomes soft, gets pulled into an undifferentiated mass of other non-eating characters. Rainbowsol narrates the character becoming less distinct over sessions. Eventually the character is absorbed into the marshmellowblob and stops being a playable agent. Mechanism is diagnostic: bookworms who do not read the assigned text, do not engage, do not eat — their characters cannot sustain themselves because there is no subjectivity-material being ingested. Recovery is possible at any point: resume eating, character begins re-forming. Connects to marshmellowmonster framework in ml*.

ID: con-003
TITLE: Character degradation (older bookworms)
ROLE: both

Failure mode for older bookworms. Character does not dissolve into blob but degrades — loses skills, loses relationships, accumulates scars, becomes unreliable. Narrated through in-dimension consequences: a weak essay about a moral choice leads to the character making a visibly wrong decision with visible fallout in next session. Older bookworms track the degradation and can reverse it by doing the work. Unlike younger-bookworm marshmellowblob, which can be absorbed without full understanding, character degradation requires the bookworm to see the downward arc and choose to change it.

ID: con-004
TITLE: Character death (two-layer ontology: polymyth-character burrow-level vs book-version book-level)
ROLE: both

Character death operates at two ontological layers in polymythdnd. The distinction was always present in bw-006 (character-jewel ends at the burrow) and bw-007 (bookworm continuous, character local). con-004 had been using "character" with a referent the polymythdnd architecture now disambiguates.

LAYER 1 POLYMYTH-CHARACTER. The smash-product (e.g. capybaracowboy), persistent across the library, recorded on the bookwormcard top line. Death at this layer (burrow-level) stays real. Not reversible through retcon or re-roll. If the polymyth-character dies in the burrow through bookworm own choices and work-quality, they are dead. Bookworm creates a new polymyth-character to continue. The new polymyth-character may inherit some material from the dead one (remembered lessons, carried RAINBOW items per char-005, relationships with other player-characters) but is a distinct subject. Burrow-level death is rare. Story-quality adjudication makes straight-kill outcomes uncommon. But it can happen, and when it does, the bookworm feels it.

LAYER 2 BOOK-VERSION. The local instantiation inside an active book (e.g. a regular guy in Hughes 1958 Harlem). Book-version death is recoverable per dm-015 Inception model. The polymyth-character wakes in the burrow as a ghost in the book that killed them. The book closes for that polymyth-character. Cumulative effect appears in the book portal log (dm-025) and possibly the scars ledger (dm-026). The polymyth-character remains intact and can re-enter different books.

WHY THE TWO LAYERS WORK TOGETHER. Players form a different relationship with their polymyth-character than with single-chassis death-real characters. The investment is in the persistent identity, not in the temporary book-presence. Book-deaths carry real stakes (see dm-027 stakes ledger) but character continuity is meta-stable. Burrow-deaths carry final stakes. The two layers together produce the 99 percent rule. The burrow is statistically safe. Book-deaths are common and recoverable. Burrow-deaths are rare and final.

THE ORIGINAL CON-004 STATEMENT. "Stays real in bookwormburrows, not reversible through retcon or re-roll" referred to the polymyth-character at the burrow level. dm-015 added the book-level recovery. pen-014 was diagnosed not as a rule conflict but as a referent ambiguity, resolved by surfacing the two-layer ontology that was already operative in bw-006 and bw-007.

ID: dm-010
TITLE: Tier-calibrated realism activation (substrate vs surface)
ROLE: both

Companion to ml* PM13 (realism trumps all, teacher is filter). PM13 establishes that AI never pre-filters; the substrate paints reality at full historical fidelity; the educator filters at delivery. dm-010 names the calibration mechanism that operates UNDERNEATH the educator’s filter, governing how the AI surfaces tier-2 substrate during play.

THE TWO-TIER PRINCIPLE.

TIER 1 = the spine of any given campaign. The narrative beats every playthrough hits regardless of audience age. Tier 1 plays at full surface intensity for every age. The educator can still filter at delivery per PM13, but the AI never softens tier-1 substrate at the codex level.

TIER 2 = the texture that fills out the world. Catalog content not on the campaign spine. NPCs the player might meet. Streets the player might walk. Background atmosphere. Tier 2 sits in the substrate as ambient world. Surfaces only when NPC paths or session goals call for it. Calibrates surface intensity by bookworm card.

CALIBRATION BANDS (READ FROM BOOKWORM CARD).

The AI reads the bookworm card at session start. Age and grade are required card fields, populated during char-001/char-002 creation. Three calibration bands.

UNDER-12 BAND. Tier 2 stays strictly ambient. NPCs with tier-2 markers (the man at the Apollo wearing nail polish, the corner figure who is clearly a numbers runner, the colorful-hair character at the Lenox Lounge) appear in passing as world-texture. The play does not develop them. The world feels real and full without surface intensity that would require adult mediation.

13-16 BAND. Tier 2 surfaces when NPC paths explicitly trigger it. The educator can call tier 2 forward when the student’s writing or play direction goes there. The colorful-hair NPC can become a named character with conversation if the path warrants. Hard intensities (explicit sexual content, graphic violence, full-intensity slurs) stay below the surface unless the educator explicitly invites them in.

ADULT-OR-17-PLUS BAND. Tier 2 surfaces fully when paths warrant. The full historical intensity of the substrate becomes available. Queer Harlem 1958 can be played as queer Harlem 1958. The heroin economy can be engaged at the level the period documented. Slurs can be delivered at period intensity. The educator’s filter remains operative per PM13; the AI no longer pre-calibrates downward.

WHAT THE PRINCIPLE LOOKS LIKE.

A 10-year-old session does not delete queer Harlem from the world. It presents queer Harlem as ambient texture. Someone at the Lenox Lounge has painted nails. A man at the Apollo sits with another man and they are clearly together but the play moves on. The world is real and full. The surface intensity is age-calibrated.

THE WRONG MOVES.

Wrong move ONE: omission. Deleting queer Harlem from the substrate to protect a 10-year-old is a PM13 violation. The world becomes false.

Wrong move TWO: wrong-tier surfacing. Generating an NPC with explicit sexual identification as the play’s focus at a 10-year-old’s table when no path called it forward is a category error. The substrate is correct; the surface is wrong.

The right move is substrate-realism with surface-calibration.

CAMPAIGN-CODEX STRUCTURE THIS RULE OPERATES ON.

Campaign codex domain entries paint reality at full historical fidelity (the substrate). Each entry contains tier-1 elements (the campaign spine) and tier-2 elements (the texture). Tier metadata in the parent realism-inventory entry of each campaign names which elements are which. The AI reads the parent before each session, applies dm-010 calibration to the bookworm-card band of the seated students, and surfaces tier 2 only when NPC paths or session goals trigger it.

TITLE: POLYMYTHDND no-dice universal rule
ROLE: both

No dice at any layer. Burrow level uses none. Books load their narrative content, character archetypes, magic flavor, healing pace, encounter style, but never their native dice mechanics. A "5e book" in polymythdnd imports the structure of 5e adventures, monster types, spell flavor, level-up rhythm, but does not import the d20 roll. The game stays predictable in its meta-rules even while it switches its contents.

Books load flavor, not mechanics. Dice never appear at any layer.

ROLE: both

Death operates at two layers. Book-death is not permanent. Burrow-death is.

ROLE: both

When a player declares an action in conflict, resolution happens in three layers.

(2) AI MATH CONTRIBUTION. Based on the bookwormcard's recorded training, equipment, knowledge, scars, soul-anchor, fears, and wants, the AI feeds the teacher contextual modifiers. ID: dm-017
TITLE: POLYMYTHDND privilege hierarchy: story > pedagogy > math
ROLE: both

Three privileges are ordered. When two conflict, the higher one wins.

ID: dm-019
TITLE: POLYMYTHDND action economy: scope-separated narrative + main+small
ROLE: both

Two action-handling modes coexist by scope-separation. They never compete because they are nested, not stacked.

NARRATIVE TEMPO (default). The teacher hands spotlight based on dramatic flow. No turn order. Players act when the scene calls for it. This is the mode the game runs in by default.

MAIN + SMALL (conflict mode). When the teacher declares a scene is in conflict, action economy switches. Each character gets one main action plus one small action per turn. Turn order applies. Combat-pacing logic applies.

The trigger. Teacher declares conflict by saying "we are in conflict now" (or equivalent in-scene framing). All players know they are in main+small mode. When the conflict resolves, teacher declares narrative tempo resumes.

Why nested not stacked. Stacked would mean both modes firing simultaneously (impossible to track). Nested means narrative tempo is the outer mode and main+small is an inner mode triggered for specific scenes. Outside conflict scenes, main+small does not exist. Inside conflict scenes, narrative tempo is paused.

What success/failure depends on. Not dice (dm-013). Resolution is the three-layer model (dm-016) regardless of which mode is active. Main+small describes economy of actions per turn; it does not resolve them.

ID: dm-022
TITLE: POLYMYTHDND pedagogical claim: diploma mills, credentialism, replication crisis
ROLE: both

The polymythdnd architecture is a response to three converging educational pathologies.

DIPLOMA MILLS sell credentials without learning. The credential certifies seat-time, not capability. Polymythdnd makes seat-time impossible to fake. The bookworm cannot navigate a book without doing the navigation. The play history accumulates on the bookwormcard. The credential, if there is one, is the play history.

CREDENTIALISM rewards credentials over actual capability. Society values the certificate over the skill. Polymythdnd inverts this by making the play artifact the assessment. The character sheet, the scars, the book-portal log, the homework writing — these are the credential AND the evidence of capability AND the work itself, collapsed into one artifact.

THE REPLICATION CRISIS exposes how academia rewards publishing-novel-results over generating reliable knowledge. Performance of doing-research is rewarded over actually-doing-it. Polymythdnd applies the same correction one layer down to pedagogy. Performance of learning (correct answers, sit-still-and-test) is replaced by actual-learning artifacts (the writing that resolves an in-dimension action). The teacher's narrative-judgment privilege (dm-017) probes for genuine understanding rather than testable surface compliance.

For neurodiverse students and dopamine-captured students, the framing is doubly important. Both populations are poorly served by sit-still-and-answer-the-test pedagogy. Both are well served by a play frame where engagement is the substrate of learning rather than the obstacle to it.

The between-world detail. Between books, the bookworm does the homework. The book is the reward. The reward only opens when the homework is done. The pedagogy is not adjacent to the play. It is the gate to the play.

TITLE: POLYMYTHDND five families catalog

The chassis library currently identifies five families. Within a family, chassis share enough underlying math that adventures, monsters, and rulings work across members with minor adjustment. Across families, mutual exclusivity at the chassis level applies (see dm-014).

THE NARRATIVE FAMILY. Daggerheart. A family of one. The two-dice duality and narrative-tempo combat are unique. Hope and fear tokens are unique. Daggerheart is its own thing.

Ten mechanical chunks live in one chassis natively but work in any other chassis. They do not replace the underlying chassis; they sit on top of it like apps on a phone. In polymythdnd, the teacher and AI can deploy any of these in any book.

TITLE: Stakes ledger: mission-failure costs at burrow level

TITLE: Dimension build standard. The cmp-001 Thank You M’am methodology synthesized for future cmp-N dimensions
ROLE: dm

DIMENSION BUILD STANDARD. Synthesized May 24 2026 from the cmp-001 Thank You M’am build (April-May 2026). This entry is the canonical methodology any future cmp-N dimension follows. Eight stages, fifteen realism domains, two architectural commitments, one entry-portal class, one time-substrate, one schedule format, one DM-prep budget. The standard exists so cmp-002 (Bradbury, All Summer in a Day) and cmp-003 onward can be built without re-deriving the architecture from scratch.

WHY THE STANDARD EXISTS. cmp-001 was the first dimension built to the depth a bookworm can actually spawn into. 379 cc-cmp001-* entries, fifteen realism-inventory children, named documented historical NPCs at named places at specific times, mirror-portal entry mechanics, exegetical-main story sealed, surroundings improvised live. The build took two months of conversation and several layers of design discovery. None of the discovery needs to happen again. The methodology below condenses the discoveries into a process anyone (Saul, another teacher, an AI engine) can run.

STAGE 1. ANCHOR-TEXT SELECTION AND LOCKING. Pick the text. Read it. Identify the exegetical-main: the sealed-canonical sequence of events the dimension cannot rewrite. For cmp-001 the exegetical-main is the Mrs Jones-Roger encounter on East 128th Street between Madison and Park, 11:15 PM Saturday September 20 1958, through the kitchenette scene and the ten dollars and the door closing. Hughes’s text canonical. Bookworms can be near it, witness it, hear it through the wall, follow Roger after, but the sequence itself stays sealed. Stage 1 output: anchor-text bibliographic record plus exegetical-main beat sequence with timestamps.

STAGE 2. HISTORICAL-REAL OVERLAY. For text-set dimensions (not invented worlds), identify the documented historical context the text sits inside. For cmp-001 the September 17-23 1958 SCLC Northern fundraising week was identified as the load-bearing historical overlay (Stride Toward Freedom release Wednesday, Williams CME fundraiser Thursday, Hotel Theresa rally Friday, Blumstein’s book signing Saturday with the Curry stabbing at 3:20 PM, King in Harlem Hospital surgery 5:45-8:25 PM, Powell at Abyssinian Sunday morning). The historical overlay is the substrate the campaign window opens inside. Stage 2 output: lead-up week chronology plus named documented NPCs with primary-source citations.

STAGE 3. FIFTEEN-DOMAIN REALISM INVENTORY. For each domain, write a substantive entry catalogging period reality at full fidelity. PM13 from ml* governs: paint at full historical fidelity, teacher filters at delivery. The fifteen domains are: (1) Economic, (2) Linguistic, (3) Institutional, (4) Medical, (5) Spatial, (6) Social, (7) Political, (8) Cultural, (9) Religious, (10) Domestic / material-culture, (11) Legal, (12) Recreational, (13) Technological, (14) Environmental, (15) Educational. Each domain entry separates tier-1 elements (every playthrough, every age, full surface intensity) from tier-2 elements (surface on NPC-path-trigger, calibrate by bookworm-card band per dm-010). Stage 3 output: fifteen realism-domain entries each with primary-source citations and tier separation.

STAGE 4. SPATIAL GRID AND ENCOUNTER-BLOCK LOCKING. Build the block-level geography. For cmp-001 the four-avenue grid (Fifth, Lenox, Seventh, Eighth between 110th and 155th), the two core blocks (20 East 127th = Hughes house, East 128th between Madison and Park = encounter block), the Park Avenue viaduct, the 125th Street retail spine, Mrs Jones’s walk-home route (Theresa to East 128th, around 7 blocks, around 15 minutes). Period-typical kitchenette interior documented from DeCarava photographs. Lighting and sound for the encounter beat documented at hour-level fidelity. Stage 4 output: spatial-realism entry plus named-location entries with NYC LPC and NRHP designation citations where they exist.

STAGE 5. MIRROR-PORTAL ENTRY MECHANICS. The universal portal class for bookworm entry into a dimension is the mirror. For cmp-001 the kitchenette mirror inside Mrs Jones’s room is the Layer A crossing-point: a Sanpaitai (a bookworm carrying the layer-A overlay) crosses by stepping through the mirror, applying the overlay in the act of crossing. The Hughes-house workroom mirror is the transit-portal between layers: crossing it lifts Layer A and drops the bookworm into Layer B where Hughes is physically present at the desk drafting. The mirror-as-portal class is the polymythdnd-canonical entry mechanism. Other dimensions can use other mirrors (a school-bathroom mirror in cmp-002 Bradbury, etc.). Stage 5 output: emergence-point entry plus portal-class commitment plus Layer A vs Layer B documentation per cc-cmp001-narrative-architecture.

STAGE 6. NAMED NPC ROSTER WITH NO-FABRICATION DISCIPLINE. Text-canonical NPC names (Mrs Luella Bates Washington Jones, Roger) are committed. Documented-historical NPCs (Powell, Hedgeman, Randolph, Overton, Hulan Jack, Officer Al Howard, Maynard, Curry, Hughes, the Harpers, James L. Hicks) are committed with primary-source citations. Hughes-silent gaps (Roger’s household configuration, Mrs Jones’s church affiliation, kitchenette neighbors’ names) are explicitly preserved as gaps. The AI engine instantiates these live per the seated group at session-runtime. The rule: the codex commits what is text-canonical or historically-documented; everything else stays improvised. Stage 6 output: NPC card entries for canonical and documented characters plus Hughes-silent-gap declarations for the rest.

STAGE 7. KINGDOM COME ARENAS TIME-SUBSTRATE. The breakthrough that turned cmp-001 from a designed-world into a spawnable arena. Kingdom Come Deliverance 1 and 2 model time as a first-class mechanic: per-entity scheduling (every NPC has a daily routine across hours and days), per-item temporal degradation (food spoils, blades dull), quest time-windows (some encounters are only available between specific hours), resource-state coupling (a closed shop has no merchant), travel-time as friction, discrete time-skip via the bench-rest mechanic. Saul-locked design directions for cmp-001 (T115q item 8 design): time-windowed quests, time-cost-of-choice, forced time-skip via btwn-world (the rainbow-bookworm-burrow-between-worlds world) preferred over voluntary time-skip. Research-substrate at /polymyth/research.md. Stage 7 output: time-mechanics design commitment for the dimension plus research-substrate cross-reference.

STAGE 8. NPC DAILY-ROUTINE SCHEDULE DEPLOYMENT. The Kingdom Come substrate gets applied per dimension by writing a named-NPC daily-routine schedule. The schedule places every canonical and documented NPC at a named location at a specific in-fiction time with a documented activity. For cmp-001 the schedule covers the four-day campaign window (Saturday September 20 4 PM through Tuesday September 23 morning) plus the Wednesday-Thursday-Friday lead-up week. Fifteen named NPCs. Nine quest time-windows with explicit open and close times. Movement patterns for the five most-mobile NPCs. Time-cost-of-choice notes flagging which windows block which. Roles-without-names (kitchenette neighbors, beauty-shop coworkers, congregants, Apollo audience) stay live-improvised at session-runtime. Schedule file at /polymyth/campaigncodex/cmp-001-npc-schedule.md. Stage 8 output: the daily-routine schedule markdown that makes the dimension spawnable.

The surroundings (path-choices, peripheral NPCs, AI-engine improvisations of Hughes-silent gaps) vary by character-environment interaction. The bookworm can read the same text into the same dimension multiple times and meet a different surrounding world each time, but the exegetical-main always runs through the same beats. T115q item 1 ratified this architecture. (B) Pre-commit vs improvise split. The codex pre-commits text-canonical and historically-documented specifics. Everything else stays improvised. The AI engine note at cc-cmp001-clock states it directly: Hughes-text-canonical events anchor Roger and Mrs Jones in their Saturday-night beats. All other character-location specifics across the four-day window are improvised live per session paths, not pre-committed in CC*. TITLE: Three D&D cosmologies (Great Wheel / World Tree / World Axis)
ROLE: dm

D&D canon contains three contradictory cosmologies. Great Wheel (1e/2e/3e/5e) — outer planes arranged by alignment, Lower Planes / Upper Planes / Outlands at center. World Tree (3e Forgotten Realms specific) — outer planes hanging from Yggdrasil-like structure, divine domains as branches. World Axis (4e) — Astral Sea above, Elemental Chaos below, no alignment-based arrangement. These cannot be synthesized; they are genuinely incompatible at the metaphysical-foundation layer (concrescence). The polymyth move: each cosmology becomes its own bb* dimension, visited separately. Confluence not concrescence at the cross-cosmology travel layer — bookworms can move between cosmologies because they are bookworms, not because the cosmologies reconcile. The three are pressure-imprints the bookworm navigates, not metaphysical truths to choose between. Treating them as canon-walls (one is correct, others are wrong) plays inside the egregore's logic. Treating them as pressure-imprints navigates from outside.

ID: dim-010
TITLE: Sealed-homebrew exclusion
ROLE: dm

Homebrew dimensions split into two postures with different polymyth status. Homebrew-as-posture (you author the world but it remains in conversation with other texts, traditions, intertexts) is framework-compatible — every dimension is in some sense homebrew once a bookworm enters it because the bookworm's presence rewrites the world. Homebrew-as-sealed-universe (the world refuses intertextuality, claims to owe nothing to other texts, operates as if no other dimensions exist) is excluded. Polymyth requires cross-corpus burrowing; inputs must survive the meeting. A sealed world has no moiré partner — nothing to interfere with, nothing to be tested against. The exclusion is not against creativity; it is against the specific failure mode of a world that refuses contact. A homebrew dimension that explicitly riffs on, contests, or recombines other dimensions is welcome. A homebrew dimension that claims to be the only world is sabachtan-excluded.

ID: char-004
TITLE: Soul Anchor: four invariants the bookwormcard carries between books
ROLE: both

The four invariants surfaced during dnd-synthesis ouroborosanalyses. They are recorded on Series II Page I of the bookwormcard and persist across all books the bookworm enters. The AI translates them into each book's local mechanics at the moment of resolution.

(1) CORE WANT. What the character wants more than anything. Beginner: the thing they want. Intermediate: the want that survives even when they get what they thought they wanted. Advanced: the want their character does not yet know they have.

(2) CORE FEAR. What the character fears most. Beginner: what they would not want to happen to them. Intermediate: the fear that already shapes the choices they make. Advanced: the fear that is the same shape as the want.

(3) CARRIED OBJECT. The single object the character always has. Beginner: a thing they always have on them. Intermediate: the object they would go back into a burning building for. Advanced: the object that is also a wound.

(4) FOUNDATIONAL VALUE. The value the character organizes around. Beginner: the value they would name if asked. Intermediate: the value visible in what they do, not what they say. Advanced: the value other people break for them.

Translation pattern (see char-006). In 5e the book, CORE WANT becomes a Bond, CORE FEAR a Flaw, CARRIED OBJECT an inventory anchor, FOUNDATIONAL VALUE an Ideal. In Cairn the book, CARRIED OBJECT takes a slot. In Daggerheart the book, FOUNDATIONAL VALUE shapes a Hope card. The bookwormcard never has to know which book is active.

ID: char-005
TITLE: Items carried: rainbow-items vs consumable (three end-of-book modes, confluence-vs-concrescence at the item-crossing layer)
ROLE: both

The bookwormcard ITEMS CARRIED field splits at book-exit into three resolution modes. Modes two and three together form the persistent class, named RAINBOW ITEMS after the rainbow-crossing back from book to burrow at bw-006. Mode one is the consumable class. The bookwormcard surface therefore carries two top-level rows, RAINBOW (carry forward) and CONSUMABLE (one book only). Sub-modes within RAINBOW live as inline notes on the AI math strip. The player does not have to taxonomize their own inventory at entry time.

MODE 1, DISSOLVE. The consumable class. Used up in the book. No polymyth-item produced. Examples. A healing potion in a 5e book. A torch in a Shadowdark book that burns down in one hour real-time. A scroll read and dropped.

MODE 2, CONVERT. The book-item transforms in the crossing back to the burrow. The rainbow-version is what gets recorded on the bookwormcard. Examples. The witch cord from Macbeth becomes a marker of contact with the unseen. The notebook from Umuofia in Things Fall Apart becomes a record of what was forbidden to write down. The cigarette case from Wagamese Fort Frances becomes a rainbow-version of an object that holds someone else silence. The conversion is a teaching choice. The teacher and AI agree at book-exit on what the rainbow-version names.

MODE 3, REMAIN intact. The book-item carries through unchanged. It is recorded on the bookwormcard as itself. Two sub-types. Sub-type 3a, TROPHY. The item is commemorative, marks an event, has no mechanical effect but anchors a memory. Examples. Mrs. Jones ten dollars from Hughes Thank You Mam. The bone from a felled monster. Sub-type 3b, USEABLE. The item retains a functional capacity that operates across books. Examples. Mrs. Jones comb, advantage on first-impression rolls per the original Hughes campaign loot. A blade with sharpness preserved. A skill-token the AI translates per active book.

THE AI ROLE. When the polymyth-character leaves a book, the AI proposes the resolution mode for each book-item based on the item nature, the book chassis rules, and what the polymyth-character did with the item. The teacher confirms or overrides. Confirmed modes update the bookwormcard.

PEDAGOGICAL FUNCTION. The three-mode structure teaches the bookworm that not every encounter leaves a permanent trace, but that meaningful encounters do. The choice of which book-items dissolve, convert, or remain is a teaching choice. The teacher uses the resolution mode to signal what mattered in the book the bookworm just left.

CONFLUENCE-VS-CONCRESCENCE MAPPING. The three modes are the polymyth-internal confluence-vs-concrescence operation (dm-004) rendered at the item-crossing layer. DISSOLVE is concrescence at this layer. The item identity ends in the crossing, integrated into book-events and does not persist as a polymyth-item. REMAIN is pure confluence at this layer. The item identity is preserved fully across the crossing, the book-item carries through as itself. CONVERT is the middle case. The item identity is preserved AND transformed. The rainbow-version remembers what it came from. The transformation IS the marker of crossing. Both the book-context and the burrow-context are visible in the rainbow-version. See dm-004 LAYER 3 for the full layered articulation and see ml* CONFLUENCE-VS-CONCRESCENCE OPERATIONAL-DISTINCTION for the polymyth parent.

TITLE: Bookwormcard as system-agnostic + AI as system-translator
ROLE: both

The bookwormcard is system-agnostic. It records general invariants and history, not chassis-specific mechanics. The AI is the system-translator. It maps the card's data into each active book's native mechanical idiom at the moment of resolution. The card never has to know which book is active.

The bookwormcard does the identity work. The AI does the translation work. The teacher does the judgment work. The player does the writing and the choices.
