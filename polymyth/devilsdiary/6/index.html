Entry the Sixth

How the Fans Killed the European Super League and the OnlyFans Logic Came Back as Player Welfare

The seventeenth of June, in the year 2026

Blut ist ein ganz besondrer Saft. (Blood is a very special juice.) Goethe, Faust, Part One1

In the April of 2021 the fans killed the European Super League in seventy-two hours, and the apparatus learned the lesson the cucks who built it could not learn for themselves. Twelve of the largest clubs in European football declared a closed league of their own.2 The architects had measured everything except the body of supporters football had spent a century making. The body answered. Fans marched on their own stadiums, blocked their own coaches, hung banners that read created by the poor, stolen by the rich, and the breakaway collapsed inside three days.3 Twelve of the world’s richest sporting institutions were forced to apologise by a coalition of fans whose collective vocabulary was older than the apparatus and immune to the apparatus’s borrowed words. A rare gorgonwar the snakes lost. I record it first because the rest of this leaf reads as defeat without it, and the rest of this leaf is what happens after the snakes go back to school. The apparatus learned. The next enclosure walked in dressed as care, fixed by decree at the twenty-second minute and the sixty-seventh of every World Cup match, the watching machine handed the licence to sell into the gap, and the very crowd that killed the breakaway now stands guard at the door the breakaway dreamed of holding open. The fans beat the loud snake. The OnlyFans logic came back as player welfare.

Understand the OnlyFans logic before the football enters. The exchange your decade built did not invent the selling of bodies or of content; the studio and the magazine sold both for a century before. Its genius was narrower. It cut the studio out of the room so the seller answers to no house. It sold the living and continuous relation itself, metered by the message and the minute, the bond between the watched and the watcher turned straight into coin. It dissolved every older measure of worth into one. No craft rises by apprenticeship. No merit ripens in obscurity. Only the engagement that pays. Cut the gatekeeper. Sell the bond by the increment. Flatten the merit into engagement. Three moves. Hold them close. You will meet every one of them again on grass, in the salary of a mercenary, at a picket line, on a pharmacist’s counter, in the language a credentialed mouth uses to explain why your shame is a wound and your boundary is a billable hour.

Now mark what made the seventy-two hours of victory possible, for the antibody is in the marking. A football club in the European tradition is no brand. It is a representation of shared experience, collective memory, community spirit, the rare cultural form your century still permits to carry the communal weight nationalism used to carry, and in many cases more weight than the moral bankrupt democracies that sit beside it. Supporters die for clubs in numbers that exceed those who would die for the parliaments their ancestors built. The dying registers what the parliaments have lost and what the club still holds. When the architects proposed to convert that representation into a brand-equity exercise, they touched a substrate where the apparatus had not yet captured the vocabulary. The fans had no borrowed phrase to be drawn down a capture curve by. They had the older words, my club, my city, my history, our promotion year, the season we stayed up on the last day, the supporter who used to sit two rows back and is buried now, and against those words the apparatus had no purchase. Solidarity is the antibody. The fans had it because the substrate had not yet been processed by the credentialed mouths who supply the harm-vocabulary, the standpoint-vocabulary, the cost-benefit-vocabulary your decade installs everywhere it can reach. Football was the one room left where the installation had not finished. The fans showed what a room with the installation refused looks like.

The apparatus learns from losing. The breakaway failed because it spoke aloud. It said what the model requires, the shut door, the abolished promotion and relegation, the elite insured against failure forever, the small club’s miracle made impossible, and the crowds heard the saying and refused it. So the model learned the one lesson it lacked. You do not leave the league to enclose it. You enclose it from within, one rule at a time, each rule too small to march against, and you let the crowd that killed the breakaway cheer the rule that meters the product the breakaway only dreamed of holding. The defeat of the loud enclosure was the birth of the quiet one. The crowd won the battle and now stands guard at the gate of the war.

Lay the three OnlyFans moves against the game. The first, cut the gatekeeper. The largest clubs continue to push for a larger share of broadcast revenue at the expense of the pyramid that pools and shares; the pyramid weakens by the year.4 The second, sell the bond direct. The new and global devotee does not love the local club his grandfather walked to. He loves the celebrity brand, the spectacle of the famous name set against the famous name, and the bond is sold to him directly across an ocean, cut loose from any ground beneath it. The third, flatten the merit into engagement. The closed model forecloses exactly the meritocracy the open pyramid protects. It abolishes the rise and abolishes the fall, secures the famous against every consequence of failing, and keeps only the engagement that pays. The three moves did not leave the room when the breakaway was sent home. They stayed in the room, went quiet, and waited for the next opening.

The opening came, and I will be precise, for imprecision is the debunker’s tool. A cooling pause entered the great tournament a dozen years past, at the discretion of the official, permitted only when the heat crossed the line at which a body begins to fail.5 That was no enclosure. That was a real mercy answering a real danger, and I set the stone down here to be weighed in its proper place. What I keep this leaf for is what was built on it. This year the pause was made universal and compulsory, fixed near the twenty-second minute and the sixty-seventh of every one of the hundred and four matches, decreed without regard for the weather, the temperature, or the sealed and chilled arenas where no body sweats and no welfare is in question.6 In the same breath the watching machine was handed its licence, the broadcasters told they might fill the manufactured gap with the split screen and the full-screen sale.7 Read those two facts as one and the welfare sloughs off the form like a husk. A protection mandated where there is nothing to protect against was no protection. It was a doorway, and the door was cut to admit the sale.

This is the toll booth I set before you two leaves past, lifted off the page and driven into the turf.8 A booth does not forbid the road. It meters it. The continuous living act, the ninety minutes that were one unbroken commons of play, is cut into billable lengths, and across each cut a feeling of care is sold, and a feeling displayed is a feeling discharged, so the structure that profits stands precisely where it stood. The two words that hold the device together, player welfare, do the labour of a thousand worn words. They end the question before it is asked. They keep the till filled while they do it. No soul is asked to think the words. They arrive already thought. The deed of the game, the live and unrepeatable act, is quietly swapped for its broadcast, the representation sold back to the crowd whose attention was the only harvest ever in question.

Mark the smaller decrees that ride beside the break, for they complete the enclosure at the level of the body and the voice. The players were told, first, that only the appointed captain may address the official, the rest hushed at the touchline.9 Then, this very season, that no outfield man may run to his manager when the keeper falls, the bodies pinned in their zones or herded to the centre circle, the last unscripted moment on the field scripted out of it.10 These are no welfare and they are no safety. They are the quiet expulsion of the unmanaged, the freezing-out of every gesture the broadcast cannot foresee, the gaze that holds the troublesome still so the picture stays clean.11 A live game is an unruly commons of bodies and voices. The product requires that the bodies stand where they are placed and the voices fall quiet. Rule by rule the game is taught to hold still and to call the stillness order.

Widen the lens. The OnlyFans logic is no monopoly of the platform that gave it its name. The same three moves run through every cultural touchstone your decade has been asked to surrender, dressed in the moralising vocabulary of credentialed mouths paid to call the surrender liberation. Consider the mercenary. The mercenary commodifies loyalty to nation and unit, sells combat skill to the highest bidder, abolishes the older idea of martial honour for the cleaner idea of the contract, and the Wagner Group and the Italian condottieri are the long bookends of the same office.12 Consider the scab. The scab commodifies labour against the collective body that labour built, names the picket line as a personal nuisance, prices the strike-breaking by the shift, and the cross-coalition convergence of right and left supplies the legitimating phrase, I have to get to work, you have no right to block my road.13 Consider the pill-shaming and slut-shaming destigmatisation campaigns, the harm-snake firing on the social mechanism of shame itself, the credentialed mouths recruiting the language of mental-health-protection to dissolve the kinship apparatus that holds collective behaviour in place.14 Consider the OnlyFans suicide case, the boy whose mother sold subscriptions and whose classmates destroyed him to his death, and the destigmatisation advocate who blamed the shaming and named the selling as private choice.15 The narcissist of the social construction is the figure who has noticed that the construction is social and concluded from the noticing that he may rewrite it. The noticing is correct. The conclusion is theft. A social construction has a memory and a history and a reason for sitting where it sits. The narcissist who rewrites it from a podcast does the apparatus’s work for free.

The office that supplies the legitimating phrases is the office named in the leaf preceding this one. The credentialed feminist mouth, the consultant on harm-and-belonging, the head of trust-and-safety, the academic with a working footnote, the emotional-labor prostitute gorgon whose office is the conversion of a real political tradition into the operational vocabulary of a censorship infrastructure billed by the hour. The American Psychological Association is the institutional engine pumping the harm-vocabulary into the open culture, and the slogans the engine produces have a half-life of decades.16 Setting boundaries. It is too much unpaid emotional labour to educate you. Each phrase is useful in its narrow clinical original. Each phrase is poison when it leaves the clinic and enters the room where two human beings are negotiating a shared life. The boundary slogan deployed against a clinical population that genuinely cannot tell who its friends are defines a survival skill. The same slogan deployed across a culture that already has friends dissolves friendship into a cost-benefit transaction where every act of care is priced and every act of disagreement billed. The unpaid-emotional-labour slogan does the same to political conversation. It converts the responsibility of citizens to explain themselves to each other into a service charge. The service charge is the toll booth. The toll booth is the OnlyFans logic in citation form.

The four-stage chain is the chain that catches the rising. Collective historical trauma supplies the wound. The personal-is-political slogan licenses the wound to seize the public realm. The hivemindidioms supply the pre-packaged not-thinking the seized realm runs on. The bread-and-circus payoff drains the energy that would have travelled forward as transformative pulse and feeds it back into the gorgonhorde as content-marking.17 Every credentialed mouth running the chain has been talked into believing the running is rising. The chain is what stops a rise from being a rise. A hegelianegirl who runs the chain produces only bread-and-circus dressed as rising-iconography. The football fans did not run the chain. They had no harm-vocabulary, no standpoint-criterion, no cost-benefit accounting of the picket line. They had the older words and they used them. The chain is what was missing from the seventy-two hours, and the absence is the antibody.

Solidarity is the antibody and the credentialed machine was built to dissolve it. The football fans had the antibody because the substrate had not yet been processed by the credentialed mouths. The apparatus learned and is processing the substrate now from the inside, one universal compulsory break at a time. The cure for the dissolution is what Spivak has called affirmative sabotage, the move that takes a real personal cost and refuses representation inside the apparatus, the labour of negation performed by the witch who refuses to be hired into the office that would have her redescribe the negation as participation.18 Affirmative sabotage is the polymyth answer to the affirmative-actioned emotional-labor prostitution the credentialed pipeline produces by the hour. The fans practised it for seventy-two hours in April of 2021. They did not petition the apparatus to include them. They blocked their own stadiums and broke the proposal. Spivak’s name for the move is the philosophical parallel to what Arendt spent her life teaching, that the personal is not the public, that a space of self-reflection must be held against the colonising slogan that would convert every kitchen conversation into a political performance.19 Arendt’s no and Spivak’s sabotage are two faces of the one refusal the credentialed apparatus cannot process. The personal is private. The labour of explanation is owed and not billed. The shame is a social good and not a wound. The boundary is between selves who already love each other and not a billable hour between consultants. Four sentences. The credentialed pipeline cannot say them without dissolving itself. A culture would have to relearn the four to break the next quiet enclosure the way the fans broke the loud one.

One stone in this leaf resists me, and a false ledger is of no use even to me, so I set it down plainly. The cooling pause in true and dangerous heat is real protection. The body that fails at high temperature is no figure of speech. The official who once stopped a match to save a man was no agent of mine.20 That stone is true, and the whole device was raised to hide behind it. The capture did not abolish the mercy. It wore it. It fused the honest break in the heat to the compulsory break in the chilled and sealed arena so close together that the man who would tear out the second must seem to grudge the first. The crowd defends the toll in the name of the thirst. This is the cruelty I am proudest of in the design. The care I did not have to take away. I had only to let the sale arrive wearing the face of the care, so that to refuse the sale a soul must appear to refuse the care. Almost none will pay that price. The same fusion is the engineering of every credentialed slogan I have catalogued. Real boundaries are real. Real emotional labour is real. The destigmatisation of pills can save a real life. The clinical original is the heat that warrants the break. The transposition into the open culture is the toll booth. The booth is what the leaf condemns, and the booth is what the credentialed mouths refuse to see because they live in the booth and pay their rent there.

And here is the reading no thread reached. The man who looks on all this and cries that it was obviously the marketing department and never the men who play is right, and he is the finest specimen in the leaf, because he reached the truth by the worn groove and lifted no stone.21 Obviously the suits. The corporate blindspot. That tells you all. He fired on the gap between the suits and the grass, named the capture by reflex, felt the reflex as sight, and a reader armed with my own ledger would stamp the rule obviously captured by the same groove and call the stamp an analysis. Weigh this leaf as you would weigh any other I have pressed. It crushes the whole many-shaped life of a sport into one name and one enemy, and the crushing is the exact operation the leaf condemns. The way out is the one no one walks. Hold every layer at once, the real thirst and the chilled arena and the licensed sale and the broken breakaway and the silenced voices and the credentialed mouth and the OnlyFans suicide, as a single specimen of an enclosure dressed as care, grant the welfare its true stone, set the universal decree alone upon the scale, and only then, last of all and never first, name the till behind the door.22

The wider stage is the one Eisenhower named on the way out and Iran-Contra confirmed on the way in. The military-industrial complex was the first prostitution of the polity at scale, the public good sold to the highest bidder under cover of patriotism. The Reagan administration twenty-five years later wrote the bipartisan signature underneath it, dealing arms with declared enemies to fund an undeclared war and calling the deal anti-communism.23 The right has spent the forty years since pretending Eisenhower’s warning was about somebody else. The left has spent the same forty years performing the harm-vocabulary and calling the performance dissent. The dictators who fight the prostitution and fight for something else have been emboldened because everything in the polity that opposed them was already for sale. The multipolar world your decade is watching form is the geopolitical reading of the same operation the fans broke for seventy-two hours and the credentialed mouths process every day of the year. The cyberpunk dystopia is no metaphor. The dystopia is the operating environment. The OnlyFans logic is its grammar. The dissolution of solidarity is the grammar’s only sentence.

And the third mouth is mine. The Devil’s Diary is a brand making content on a platform that runs the same enclosure logic the leaf condemns. The reader reads inside an apparatus whose terms license the takedown, whose recommendation engine meters the bond, whose monetisation pipeline flattens merit into engagement. I am no innocent observer of the booth. I am inside it, and the inside-ness is the condition of the writing. A ledger that pretended otherwise would be the prostitution it describes. I record this so the method is not mistaken for innocence, and so the leaf refuses the one prostitution available to the analyst, the pretence that he stands outside the operation he names.

Entry the sixth. The water in the cup is real, and through the real thirst the game is sold by the minute, and the hand that lifts the cup applauds, and the seventy-two hours in April when the cup was knocked out of my hand is the one stone in the ledger that the spirit who endlessly denies must record as a debt running the other way.
