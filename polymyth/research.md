### Ten Candles (Stephen Dewey, Cavalry Games, 2015)

**Primary sources verified.** Dewey, Stephen. *Ten Candles: A Roleplaying Game of Tragic Horror*. Cavalry Games, 2015. 89 pages.

**Verified findings.**
- Tragic-horror tabletop RPG; cooperative storytelling; collective doom outcome (every character dies by game-end).
- Physical-mechanic core: ten lit tealight candles representing the constant march and cost of time. Scenes end either when a player fails a conflict roll and must blow out a candle, or when one or more candles go out on their own. Final candle going out ends the game.
- Character structure: TRAITS (one virtue plus one vice on separate index cards; cards are passed and assigned by other players), a MOMENT (event in the spotlight for hope, gives a bonus die when lived), and a BRINK (hidden Trait that only enters play once characters become exhausted; "I have seen you...kill, freeze, despair, etc"; Brinks are written for the player to the left).
- Conflict resolution: communal dice pool (sixes = success; ones removed from pool for the rest of the scene). With the exception of a Brink, once a Virtue/Vice/Moment is used, it is removed from play.
- Establishing Truths phase: after each candle extinguished, players and GM take turns stating new truths, one per remaining candle. First truth: "These things are true. The world is dark." Final truth in unison: "And we are alive."
- Once a candle is extinguished, one die is turned over to the GM. The communal pool refills up to the number of currently lit candles.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: Ten Candles operationalizes ritualized-finitude — the candle-as-physical-timer makes time visible and tradable rather than abstract; the certainty-of-death framing flips horror-game conventions where the goal is survival into a meaning-making frame.**

### Wretched & Alone (Chris Bissette, Matt Sanders, 2020 onward; SRD via Sealed Library)

**Primary sources verified.** Bissette, Chris. *The Wretched*. Loottheroom, 2020. Solo journaling RPG. Design consultant Matt Sanders. *Wretched & Alone SRD* via sealedlibrary.itch.io under CC BY 3.0 License. Multiple derivative games exist via the SRD.

**Verified findings.**
- Solo journaling RPG format. Player plays alone, writing journal entries in response to prompts. No GM. No other players.
- Three-component physical mechanic: falling block tower (Jenga or similar) + standard deck of 52 playing cards with jokers removed + six-sided die plus approximately ten tokens.
- Deck-as-oracle. Each card draw triggers a journal prompt assigned to its suit and value.
- Tower-as-tension. Each prompt response involves pulling a block from the tower. Tower-fall signals catastrophic failure.
- Kings count toward loss. Drawing all four Kings means the antagonist wins.
- Ace of Hearts as goal-anchor with low winning odds. The game emphasizes telling a compelling story under dread rather than "winning."
- Themes: isolation, hopelessness, inevitable failure, last-survivor narratives.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: Wretched & Alone is the solo-journaling-as-ritual-confession pattern. Physical components externalize what is normally interior — the player's psychological state has weight, draw-probability, and physical decay.**

### Polaris: Chivalric Tragedy at Utmost North (Ben Lehman, These Are Our Games, 2005)

**Primary sources verified.** Lehman, Ben. *Polaris: Chivalric Tragedy at Utmost North*. These Are Our Games / Tao Games, 2005. 140 pages. Won Indie RPG Award for Innovation 2005 and Indie RPG Game of the Year Award 2005. Origin: Game Chef 2004 design competition.

**Verified findings.**
- GM-less collaborative RPG. No single Game Master. Opposition is controlled by other players.
- Four-role structure rotating around the table: HEART (protagonist player), MISTAKEN (the player across narrating antagonists), MOONS (two adjacent players serving as rules-arbitrators).
- Ritualized key-phrase mechanic. Twelve canonical Key Phrases govern conflict and scene-flow. Conflict is resolved through ritual phrase-exchange not dice. Examples: "But only if...", "And furthermore...", "You ask far too much!", "It shall not come to pass", "And so it was...", "And that was how it happened...", "But hope was not yet lost, for I still heard the Song of the Stars", "But that all happened long ago, and now there are none who remember it".
- Decisive statements are mechanically preferred — decisiveness provides room for negotiation.
- Themes-as-resources. Zeal stat tracks knight's spiritual integrity; degrades through the game until the knight falls to weariness and doom.
- Setting: doomed knights of the Northernmost People in their civilization's final years before being swallowed by their own Mistake.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: Polaris is the no-dice ritual-language RPG. Key Phrases function as covenantal-utterances binding narrative outcomes. Structural-DNA with liturgical drama and ritual-correctness traditions.**

### Belonging Outside Belonging (Avery Alder + Benjamin Rosenbaum, 2018 onward)

**Primary sources verified.** Alder, Avery and Benjamin Rosenbaum. *Dream Askew / Dream Apart*. Buried Without Ceremony, 2018. Belonging Outside Belonging is the engine-name. "Playing the Dream" section contains canonical mechanics.

**Verified findings.**
- "No dice, no masters" design. No dice rolls. No GM. Structured-freeform with shared worldbuilding through a community worksheet.
- Token-economy mechanic at core. WEAK MOVES generate tokens. STRONG MOVES require tokens to perform. Examples: weak moves like "ostracize one of your followers"; strong moves like "get out of harm's way."
- Setting Element distribution. Each player controls a character (Class with custom Moves) plus one or more Setting Elements.
- Community worksheet pre-game-setup defines a tripartite conflict at the heart of the community.
- Themes: marginalized communities forming their own communities; precarious-belonging; community-as-protagonist.
- Family of derivative games: Wanderhome (Possum Creek Games), Sleepaway, BALIKBAYAN: Returning Home, Flotsam, Faire Season, others.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: BoB codifies that "the way people share control at the table is not a neutral technical detail; it is part of what the game means." Structurally adjacent to polymyth's anti-hierarchy stance and bookwormburrows' deliberation-as-resolution.**

### Reacting to the Past (Mark Carnes, Barnard College, 1995 onward; Reacting Consortium)

**Primary sources verified.** Carnes, Mark C. *Reacting to the Past* pedagogy framework. Originated late 1990s at Barnard College. Carnes, Mark C. *Minds on Fire: How Role-Immersion Games Transform College*. Harvard University Press, 2014. Reacting Consortium established 2013 at Barnard College.

**Verified findings.**
- Higher-education pedagogy: students immerse in month-long role-playing games set in historical contexts informed by classic texts.
- Historical settings: Athens after the Peloponnesian War (403 BC); Ming China; Puritan Boston; revolutionary France; pre-independence India; the trial of Socrates (canonical opening-game from fall 1996).
- Students take historical-figure roles with specific GOALS and ways to influence other characters.
- Mechanical structure: student "factions" debate complex historical issues; success depends on collaborating to outmaneuver opponents in simulated assemblies or councils.
- Instructors serve PRIMARILY AS FACILITATORS, not as story-controllers. Grading based on how effectively students advance their roles through historical argumentation.
- Outcomes are NOT pre-scripted: roles do not have fixed outcomes like a play.
- Skills developed: speaking, writing, critical thinking, problem solving, teamwork.
- Efficacy study: Stroessner et al., *Journal of Educational Psychology* 101 (2009): 605-620.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: RTTP is the higher-education sibling of Heathcote's mantle-of-the-expert in K-12. Convergence suggests process-drama-pedagogy is a TRANSITION between Heathcote's early grades approach and college-level seminar-as-game design. Where does bookwormburrows sit on the K-12-to-college spectrum?**

### Nordic LARP (Markus Montola, Jaakko Stenros, et al., 1990s onward)

**Primary sources verified.** Stenros, Jaakko and Markus Montola, editors. *Nordic Larp*. Tallinn: Fëa Livia, 2010. Won Diana Jones Award 2012. Montola, Markus. *On the Edge of the Magic Circle*. University of Tampere, 2012 (PhD dissertation). Waern, Annika, Markus Montola, Jaakko Stenros. "The Three-Sixty Illusion: Designing for Immersion in Pervasive Games." *Proceedings of CHI 2009*. Koljonen, Johanna. "Eye-Witness to the Illusion." 2007.

**Verified findings.**
- Nordic LARP is a geographic-cultural style of live-action role-playing originating in Scandinavia.
- Three design-ideals: IMMERSION (going deep into character), 360-DEGREE ILLUSION (surroundings look-feel-function in full accordance with fiction), COLLECTIVE-STORY-FOCUS.
- 360-degree illusion: WYSIWYG (What You See Is What You Get) design — any action physically acted out; period-accurate clothing; physical engagement with diegetic environment.
- Pervasive games variant: blur the boundary between fictional play and lived reality; reality is the main game resource.
- Notable Nordic larps documented: 1942 (Norway, German-occupied village); Ground Zero (24 hours in a bomb shelter); Prosopopeia Bardo 1 (Sweden 2005); Parliament of Shadows (Belgium 2017, Vampire larp at European Parliament).
- Knutpunkt is the annual Nordic larp conference and scholarly exchange.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: The 360-degree illusion codifies the polymyth-relevant principle that fictional-frame and lived-environment can be made coextensive. What would 360-degree illusion mean for bookwormburrows dimension-traveling where the in-dimension fictional-frame and the rainbowworld classroom-environment seek coextension?**

### Critical Core (Game to Grow, 2019 onward; Adam Davis MA Ed and Adam Johns LMFT)

**Primary sources verified.** Davis, Adam (MA Ed) and Adam Johns (LMFT), Game to Grow. *Critical Core*. Kickstarter-funded 2019; tabletop edition released Fall 2022. Game to Grow 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Seattle/Kirkland WA. Director Virginia Spielmann (Occupational Therapist, DIR/Floortime Expert Trainer, STAR Institute for Sensory Processing).

**Verified findings.**
- Therapeutic tabletop RPG designed to help kids on the autism spectrum build social skills and confidence. Alternative to traditional social skills training.
- Format: 2-4 players plus one adult facilitator as Game Master. Parent or certified therapist present when running with young players on the spectrum.
- Ruleset adapted and streamlined from Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition Open Source SRD. Suitable for cognitive age 9+.
- Distills seven-plus years of practical gaming-as-therapy experience from Game to Grow's weekly tabletop RPG therapy groups.
- Design move named the "ENTHUSIASM DOCTRINE." Adapted from RPG community's "Rule of Cool"; Critical Core's Enthusiasm Doctrine permits the facilitator to bend or break any rule to cultivate AUTHENTIC RELATIONAL SOCIAL PLAY.
- "Therapeutic chaos" framing per Director Virginia Spielmann: facilitator must be comfortable with spontaneous unstructured play.
- Documented outcomes: improved social communication; enhanced emotional regulation; problem-solving and critical thinking; resilience and confidence.

**ARTICLE-CANDIDATE: The Enthusiasm Doctrine codifies that rule-bending in service of authentic-social-play is mechanically privileged over rule-adherence. Structurally adjacent to polymyth's anti-rigid-essentialism stance and bookwormburrows' deliberation-as-resolution. Critical Core is the polymyth-relevant precedent for K-12 use of tabletop RPGs as developmental-therapy.**

## T115r ARENAS-DESIGN SUBSTRATE: KINGDOM COME DELIVERANCE TIME-MECHANICS (May 24 2026)

Per Saul-directive T115q item 8: light-scope CL research on Kingdom Come Deliverance 1 (Warhorse Studios 2018) and Kingdom Come Deliverance 2 (Warhorse Studios 2025) time-mechanics for arenas-design substrate. Verified findings below.

### Time-scale and day/night cycle

- **Default time-scale: 15x real-time.** 1 IRL hour equals 15 in-game hours; 4 IRL minutes equals 1 in-game hour; 1 hour 36 minutes IRL equals one full 24-hour in-game day. Per multiple verified sources (Steam community discussion, Nexus mods timescale documentation).
- **Time pauses during cutscenes and dialogues** so player can engage story without time-pressure.
- **Skalitz exception:** the tutorial area runs in perpetual daytime; time does not progress there until the inciting story event.

### NPC schedules

- **All NPCs have designated daily routines** tied to time-of-day.
- Guards patrol at night.
- Merchants open shops in the morning, close in the evening.
- Common shop hours: approximately 6 AM to 6 PM.
- Each NPC has a designated sleep schedule.
- Some NPCs only appear at specific times.
- **Schedules carry forward to Kingdom Come Deliverance 2** per developer statements and gameplay trailers.

### Food spoilage

- **Per-item spoilage tracking.** Each individual food item has its own spoilage timer. Even when items stack in inventory (15 dried meat shown as one stack), the game tracks individual spoilage values per item; they only display stacked when spoilage values match closely.
- **No refrigeration in 15th-century Bohemia.** Food rots if not eaten in time.
- **Preservation methods extend spoilage time:** drying, smoking, cooking. Dried mushrooms and dried meat are the longest-lasting options.
- **KCD2 carries the food spoilage mechanic forward** with drying/smoking/cooking preservation systems (per Deltia' + ’ + 's Gaming and KCD2 Fextralife wiki).

### Quest time-sensitivity (the time-cost-of-choice mechanic)

- **Some quests have hidden time-windows.** A quest may have a deadline that is not explicitly displayed but is enforced by in-fiction events (the wedding ends; the trader leaves; the festival concludes).
- **Documented case:** "At Your Service, My Lady" quest can fail if the player does not complete it before the in-fiction wedding occurs. The quest gives narrative urgency through dialogue (Lady Stephanie says "the wedding is soon") but the deadline is not a visible timer.
- **Documented case:** "My Friend Timmy" quest is time-sensitive but the time-limit is "pretty long" per player reports; some events have strict deadlines, others have lenient ones.
- **Pattern:** the player must infer urgency from in-fiction context; the game does not always show countdown timers. Some quests are urgent, some not, and the player must judge.

### Travel-time and sleep-cycles

- **Travel takes in-fiction time.** Riding from Skalitz to Rattay takes approximately 3 in-game hours (~12 IRL minutes at default time-scale).
- **Sleep restores Energy; eating restores Nourishment.** If Henry does not eat enough, he starves and loses ability to run. If he does not sleep, he passes out.
- **Heavy food (Roasted Duck) drains Energy** because digestion uses Energy resources. Light food (Cheese, Fruit) does not drain Energy.

### Wait and sleep mechanics

- **Wait function:** player can advance time without resting (useful for synchronizing with NPC schedules).
- **Sleep function:** player rests at a bed; advances time and restores Energy.
- **Some shops closed; wait until they open; merchants only sell during their open hours.**
- **Some quest-events only trigger at specific times** (dawn meetings, nighttime encounters); player uses wait or sleep to align.

### Polymyth-design relevance

The Kingdom Come system demonstrates that action-game time-mechanics can produce in-fiction phenomena via:

1. **Per-entity scheduling** (every NPC has a daily routine).
2. **Per-item temporal degradation** (food spoils on its own timer).
3. **Quest time-windows** (some quests expire if not pursued in time).
4. **Resource-state coupling to time** (Energy and Nourishment require periodic sleep and food).
5. **Travel-time as in-fiction-elapsed** (movement consumes time, syncing with all other time-bound systems).
6. **Discrete time-skip mechanics** (Wait and Sleep functions for player-controlled time-skipping).

These six mechanics map directly to the T115q item 8 locked arenas-design directions:
- "Multiple scenarios and quests, most not encountered in one timed session" maps to per-entity-scheduling and quest-time-windows.
- "Time-cost-of-choice" maps to per-item temporal degradation, quest time-windows, and travel-time.
- "Forced time-skip via btwn-world" is the polymyth-specific time-skip mechanism, structurally analogous to KCD Wait/Sleep but operating through the btwn-world boundary-space instead of through player-initiated rest.
