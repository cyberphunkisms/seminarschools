Filed 2026-06-06. Third research file for the Studio Qibla world (cmp-003). Companions: cmp-003-research-films.md (24-film dossier corpus), cmp-003-research-substrate.md (Japan world-bible). ## SECTION 1. THE MAKER-PLANE GEOGRAPHY (the portal location)

STUDIO BUILDINGS, KOGANEI, TOKYO. Studio Ghibli Inc. founded 15 June 1985 by Miyazaki, Takahata, and Suzuki with Tokuma Shoten backing after Nausicaa (1984). First offices near Kichijoji Station, Musashino (2nd Ino Building). Present headquarters at 1-4-25 Kajino-cho, Koganei-shi, Tokyo, occupied 6 August 1992, roughly eight minutes on foot from Koganei Station. Nibariki (Miyazaki's personal company) and the numbered studio buildings (Studio 7 housed the Kaguya production) sit in this complex. ACCESS: PRIVATE working buildings. Fine-grained interior detail is sparse in English sources.

SAYAMA HILLS / TOTORO FOREST, TOKOROZAWA. The Sayama Hills straddle the Tokyo-Saitama border about 40 km from central Tokyo, roughly 11 km east-west and 4 km north-south around Lake Sayama and Lake Tama. The Totoro village landscape was modeled here. The Totoro no Furusato Foundation (Totoro Hometown Fund) founded April 1990 to preserve the hills; Miyazaki an early major donor and member. Totoro's Forest No. 1 purchased 1991; 55th forest by January 2020; 66 sites by early 2025.
- KUROSUKE HOUSE: the foundation's visitor base, a 100-plus-year-old former tea-farmer folk house at 351 Kamiyamaguchi, Tokorozawa, registered National Tangible Cultural Property, public since 2011. Roughly 3,000 square meters: main farmhouse, storehouse with a Totoro mark near the roof, tea factory (chakoba), all over a century old. Contains a two-meter Totoro figure, verandah, hearth, hand water pump, an Inari shrine, a well, a stream, a bamboo grove. ACCESS: PUBLIC, reservation required via totoro.or.jp, admission 500 yen funding conservation, open limited days (roughly Tue/Wed/Sat or Wed/Sat/Sun, 10:00-15:00). No parking; Seibu Ikebukuro Line to Kotesashi Station then bus toward Waseda University, alight Dainichido. Post-pandemic restriction reported: visitors asked not to share location photos online.
- Trails pass Lake Sayama, Mikajima Inari shrine, a Totoro-shaped tree, Sayama Prefectural Nature Park. ACCESS: PUBLIC open natural area.
- FLAG: the "Yamato River" reference in earlier briefs appears to conflate; the relevant watercourses are Sayama Hills streams and the two reservoirs. Verify against the world-bible before instantiating.

GHIBLI MUSEUM, MITAKA. Inokashira Park, 1-1-83 Shimorenjaku, Mitaka. Planning 1998, construction March 2000, opened 1 October 2001. Official name Mitaka City Animation Museum. Miyazaki designed it from storyboard-style cross-sections; influence from the Italian hilltop town Calcata. Manifesto: more like a house than a building; motto "Let's get lost together"; no fixed route; no interior photography. Features: central iron spiral staircases, the five-room "Where a Film is Born" studio mock-up, basement Saturn Theater with exclusive shorts (Mei and the Kittenbus), Catbus playroom (children 12 and under), Tri Hawks reading room (opened 6 Feb 2002), Mamma Aiuto shop, Straw Hat Cafe, rooftop garden with the five-meter Robot Soldier by Kunio Shachimaru and the Laputa keystone. ACCESS: PUBLIC, advance reservation (Lawson Ticket), no interior photography, no re-entry. About 15 minutes on foot from Mitaka Station (JR Chuo) along the Tamagawa Josui canal.

GHIBLI PARK, NAGAKUTE, AICHI. Inside Expo 2005 Aichi Commemorative Park (Moricoro Park); 7.1 ha complete; Goro Miyazaki director; deliberately no large rides. Phase 1 opened 1 November 2022: Ghibli's Grand Warehouse, Hill of Youth, Dondoko Forest (containing the pre-existing Satsuki and Mei's House). Mononoke Village opened 1 November 2023 (Tatara-ba recreation, Okkoto-nushi slide). Valley of Witches opened 16 March 2024 (Howl castle replica about 16 m, Kiki's home, Earwig house). ACCESS: PUBLIC, reservation-only, photography permitted. Linimo to Ai-Chikyuhaku-kinen-koen Station.

HINGE RECOMMENDATION. The in-game "find the maker's home/studio" objective resolves at the Sayama Hills / Kurosuke House (publicly visitable, reservation-gated, sensory-rich: well, Inari shrine, hearth, tea factory, bamboo grove) as the narratively satisfying and ethically clean portal, NOT the private Koganei studio.

## SECTION 2. PER-FILM CANONICAL THRESHOLDS

Public-domain foundations:
- TORII AS KEKKAI (sacred boundary): marks transition from zokukai (secular world) to shinkai (divine realm); attested mid-Heian, mentioned in a text of 922 CE; oldest extant stone torii 12th century (Hachiman shrine, Yamagata). Documentation: Aston, Shinto (1905, PD); the Engishiki.
- SHINBOKU (sacred tree): tree as shintai where kami dwell, marked with shimenawa; camphor, cryptomeria, ginkgo common. Nihon Shoki records Susano-o creating cedar, cypress, black pine, camphor from his body hair. PD folklore.
- WELLS, TUNNELS, MOUNTAIN PASSES: Tono Monogatari (Yanagita 1910) is saturated with mountain-pass (toge) boundary crossings between human and yokai worlds.
- MA: the charged interval/threshold in Japanese spatial culture.

## SECTION 3. PUBLIC-DOMAIN BESTIARY SOURCES, TRANSLATION-GATED

THE TRANSLATION-COPYRIGHT PRINCIPLE. A 10th-century tale is public domain; a modern English translation is a separate copyrighted work. Only translations published before 1929 (US cutoff) are reliably free. Verbatim-quotable game text uses pre-1929 translations; newer scholarly translations are cite-and-paraphrase only.

- KOJIKI (712). Compiled by O no Yasumaro from Hieda no Are's recitations, by order of Empress Genmei. Text PD. Translation: Basil Hall Chamberlain, "A Translation of the Ko-ji-ki," Transactions of the Asiatic Society of Japan Vol. 10 Supplement, 1882: PUBLIC DOMAIN, verbatim-quotable (Wikisource, sacred-texts). Philippi 1968 and Heldt 2014 in copyright, cite only. Game-usable: Izanagi/Izanami creation; Yomi descent and the yomotsu-shikome; Amaterasu and the cave; Susano-o and Yamata-no-Orochi; Okuninushi and the white hare of Inaba.
- NIHON SHOKI / NIHONGI (720). Text PD. Translation: W. G. Aston, Nihongi, Transactions and Proceedings of the Japan Society Supplement I, 1896: PUBLIC DOMAIN, verbatim-quotable. Value: multiple variant versions of each myth; Susano-o tree-creation; kami genealogies.
- KONJAKU MONOGATARISHU (after 1120). Anonymous compiler; 31 volumes, 28 extant, 1000-plus setsuwa from India, China, Japan; every tale opens "ima wa mukashi." Text PD. NO pre-1929 complete English translation exists. Ury 1979/1985, Jones 1959, Kelsey 1982 all in copyright: CITE AND PARAPHRASE ONLY. Game-usable: oni, tengu, kitsune, flying hermits, vengeful animals, bandits; source of Akutagawa's Rashomon and In a Grove.
- UGETSU MONOGATARI (1776). Ueda Akinari; nine supernatural tales. Text PD. No safe pre-1929 English translation identified; Zolbrod 1974 and Chambers 2007 in copyright: cite/paraphrase. Game-usable: Asaji ga Yado (the waiting ghost-wife); Aozukin (the cannibal monk); Jasei no In (the serpent-woman); the monk-becomes-carp.
- TAKETORI MONOGATARI (10th c.). The Kaguya source. PD. Pre-1929 translation: F. V. Dickins (1888).
- HEARN, KWAIDAN (1904). PD (Hearn died 1904). Yuki-onna, Mujina (the noppera-bo), Rokurokubi, Mimi-nashi Hoichi.
- SEKIEN'S FOUR CATALOGS (1776-1784). Images and original text PD. Davisson modern translations (2016-2017) in copyright.
- YANAGITA, TONO MONOGATARI (1910). Text PD in the US as pre-1929; under Japan's life-plus-70 (Yanagita died 1962) the position differs. Morse translations (1975/2008/2015) and the Mizuki manga (2010, trans. 2021) in copyright. Game-usable: kappa, zashiki-warashi, Oshira-sama, yama-haha, kamikakushi cases.

## SECTION 4. FIVE PERIOD DOSSIERS, PLAYABILITY DETAIL

(A) SENGOKU TATARA COUNTRY, c. 1500s (Mononoke). Tatara ironworkers received a rice allotment of 15 cups (go) per person per day plus cash wages by job (Tetsu no Michi heritage sources, Shimane). The murage directed a three-day-three-night smelting cycle; about 11 workers fed iron sand and charcoal; yamako charcoal-makers built clay kilns with meter-thick walls over half a month; one cycle needed about 15 metric tons of charcoal; firing 1.8 tons needed 40 people, gathering the wood 14. Rice supplemented by millet/barley. Sengoku (c. 1467-1600) decentralized warfare: harvest shares to the daimyo, ashigaru conscription, domain checkpoints, bandit passes. Iron demand recycled even nails and broken sword fragments. Kanayago-kami, the iron-making deity said to reside in a katsura tree, governed the tatara; Yamata-no-Orochi regionally tied to Izumo iron country.

(B) HEIAN COURT, c. 900-1100 (Kaguya). By the year 1000 the government had effectively ceased issuing currency; rice the primary unit of exchange, silk and pottery also traded (Farris economic history, confirm at primary). Food: rice staple (white for nobles, millet/barley substitutes in famine), fish, shellfish, seaweed, beans, melons, chestnuts, mushrooms, occasional game; chopsticks displaced the spoon after the 9th century; Buddhist meat-avoidance. Heian-kyo a planned, largely unwalled grid; provincial travel slow and dangerous. Roughly 5,000 ranked aristocrats (Ryomin) over a population near five million; clothing rank-regulated. Farris characterizes the capital as an unhygienic cesspool; epidemics and famines left bodies in streets (790: 80,000 reported starving in Kyushu); peasants in pit dwellings with straw bedding. Elite children: calligraphy, Chinese classics, poetry; peasant children worked land.

(C) TAISHO-SHOWA INTERWAR, 1912-1937 (The Wind Rises; Poppy Hill ancestry). Industrialization, Western dress alongside kimono, expanding rail, the engineer class (Horikoshi's milieu); tuberculosis a structuring fear (and Miyazaki family fact). THIS DOSSIER IS THIN IN THIS ARCHIVE; the world-bible's interwar dossier (1927 shop list: coffee 10 sen, soba 8-10 sen, rent 20 yen, graduate salary 80 yen monthly) carries the daily texture.

(D) THE 1945 HOME FRONT (Grave of the Fireflies; Totoro adjacency). Rationing (from 1940) covered rice, vegetables, sugar, seafood, dairy, clothes, nails, needles, bandages, shoes, sake, cooking oil. A child's total daily ration fell from 19.2 oz (1943) to 14.4 oz (1945). Adult meat 1.3-1.8 oz and fish 1.8 oz daily, falling toward about 1,793 calories by 1945; average per-capita intake from about 2,000 (pre-Pearl Harbor) to about 1,680 by summer 1945. Queues, black market, kimono-for-vegetables barter (the Fireflies dynamic). Tonarigumi neighborhood associations of 5-10 households distributed rations, enforced air defense and savings drives, watched neighbors, could deny rations to evacuees. Tokyo firebombing night of 9-10 March 1945 (Operation Meetinghouse): 334 B-29s, about 1,665 tons of incendiaries, an estimated 100,000 dead, over one million homeless, the single most destructive air raid in history. National civilian tolls: USSBS about 806,000 casualties including 330,000 deaths; Dower (Cultures of War) about 430,000 air-raid deaths. Roughly 8.5 million civilians displaced 1943-45. Schoolchildren grades 3-6 evacuated (87 percent by April 1945); high-school girls on 12-hour munitions and balloon-factory shifts with malnutrition, TB, rickets. The Kobe raid underlies Nosaka's 1967 novel.

(E) 1963-64 MODERNIZATION. Annualized GNP growth near 10 percent 1957-1973 (Cambridge History: real GNP about 9.6 percent annually 1946-73). Japan acceded to the OECD 28 April 1964, the 21st member and first from Asia-Pacific (MOFA). Tokaido Shinkansen opened 1 October 1964; Tokyo Olympics opened 10 October 1964; Meishin Expressway sections from 1963. Consumer aspiration shifted to the three Cs: car, cooler (air conditioner), color TV (Toyota corporate history), succeeding the three sacred treasures; by 1970 nearly a third of non-agricultural households owned a car. Tokyo a construction site; Olympic-prep water shortages (restricted bathhouse hours, emergency wells); the Showa 40 (1965) recession followed the boom peak.

## SECTION 5. MIYAZAKI AND TAKAHATA AS DOCUMENTED FIGURES (maker-plane NPC dossiers)

HAYAO MIYAZAKI, b. 5 January 1941, Tokyo (Bunkyo). Second of four brothers. Father Katsuji directed Miyazaki Airplane, a family firm making fighter-plane parts (the aviation root). Family evacuated to Utsunomiya then Kanuma 1944-46. Mother Yoshiko suffered spinal tuberculosis c. 1947-1955, hospitalized about three years then bedridden: the documented basis for the Totoro mother. Inspired by Toei's Hakujaden (1958). Gakushuin University, political science/economics, graduated 1963. Joined Toei Animation April 1963 (19,500 yen salary, 6,000 yen rent, 4.5-tatami Nerima room) as in-betweener. Chief secretary of Toei's labor union 1964 with Takahata as vice-chairman: the start of the collaboration. Key animator on Takahata's Horus (1968), mentored by Yasuo Otsuka. Married animator Akemi Ota 1965; sons Goro and Keisuke. Left for A-Pro August 1971 (Lupin Part I with Takahata); Zuiyo/Nippon Animation (Heidi 1974); Future Boy Conan 1978; Castle of Cagliostro 1979. Ghibli founded June 1985; through The Boy and the Heron 2023 (second Oscar). DOCUMENTED WORK HABITS: no script, films built entirely from hand-drawn storyboards, production starting before storyboards finish; desk in the corner among the animators; home within walking distance; the resident cat Ushiko, Yakult delivery, group calisthenics; self-described man of the twentieth century; deeply affected by Fukushima. Source: The Kingdom of Dreams and Madness (dir. Mami Sunada, 2013).

ISAO TAKAHATA, 1935 to 5 April 2018. Co-founder, the studio's other director: Fireflies 1988, Only Yesterday 1991, Pom Poko 1994, Yamadas 1999, Kaguya 2013. Famously slow, a non-drawer who forced formal innovation; documented tensions with Miyazaki (the month-long safflower scene; Kaguya overruns). Miyazaki's emotional eulogy 2018.

TOSHIO SUZUKI, b. 1948. Producer; former Animage editor at Tokuma who pushed Miyazaki to the Nausicaa manga and film; the organizational glue and dealmaker.

CORPORATE. Founded 15 June 1985 (Tokuma subsidiary); independent from Tokuma April 2005. On 21 September 2023 Nippon Television Holdings announced acquisition of 42.3 percent of Ghibli shares, becoming largest shareholder (effective about 6 October 2023); Suzuki chairman, Miyazaki honorary chairman, Hiroyuki Fukuda president; stated reason succession (Goro declined to lead).

CITABLE ENGLISH SOURCES. Miyazaki, Starting Point: 1979-1996 (trans. Cary and Schodt; VIZ 2009; ISBN 978-1-4215-0594-7), IN COPYRIGHT. Miyazaki, Turning Point: 1997-2008 (VIZ 2014; ISBN 978-1-4215-6090-8), IN COPYRIGHT. The Kingdom of Dreams and Madness (2013). Companions: Isao Takahata and His Tale of the Princess Kaguya (2013); Never-Ending Man (2016, Arakawa). ## SECTION 6. JAPANESE WORD-MAGIC AND NAME-MAGIC (sourced stack for the spell/name mechanics)

KOTODAMA (word-spirit). Words contain spiritual power; good words bring good, ominous words ill. Earliest attestation: the Man'yoshu (compiled after 759 CE, PD), which calls Japan "kotodama no sakiwau kuni," the land where the workings of language bring bliss; poems by Kakinomoto no Hitomaro (3254) and Yamanoue no Okura (894). Related: kotoage (invoking word power), kotomuke (subjugation through words), jumon (incantation). The kami Kotoshironushi appears in Kojiki/Nihon Shoki. Revived politically in WWII (kokutai linkage). Tradition and primary source PD.

IMINA (the true-name taboo). The imina is the real personal name, from imi (avoidance/taboo) plus na (name): a name to be avoided. Contrasts azana (public courtesy name) and okurina (posthumous name). Addressing a living person by imina was permissible only for parents or one's lord; samurai imina conferred at genpuku, often sharing a character (henki) with father or lord. Revealing one's true name functioned as submission or intimacy. Scholarly anchor: Herbert E. Plutschow, Japan's Name Culture (Japan Library/Curzon 1995; ISBN 1-873410-42-5), IN COPYRIGHT, cite only. Chinese underlay: bihui naming taboo; precise sinological citation PENDING.

NORITO (ritual prayers). Shinto liturgical incantations to kami; documented in Kojiki and Nihongi; the Engishiki (927 CE) preserves 27 representative norito, TEXT PD. Etymology links noru (declare), inoru (pray), norou (curse): word-magic by declaration. Translation: Donald L. Philippi, Norito (Kokugakuin 1959; Princeton 1990, ISBN 0-691-01489-2), IN COPYRIGHT, cite/paraphrase.

WRITTEN TALISMANS. Ofuda/shinsatsu (per Encyclopedia of Shinto, Kokugakuin, entry by Okada Yoshiyuki): thaumaturgic talismans of wood, paper, or metal bearing the written name of a deity, enshrined in the kamidana or affixed to gates and pillars; the written name carries the kami's power (material kotodama). Omamori: the portable mamorifuda in a brocade bag; the inner inscribed slip is the sacred object; opening discouraged. Goshuin: vermilion seal plus calligraphy with date and shrine/temple name, collected in a goshuincho; strongest origin theory a receipt for dedicated sutra-copying; described devotionally as the deity's bunshin (divided body). ORIGIN CONTESTED, flag. Scholarly anchor for lived talisman practice: Ian Reader, Religion in Contemporary Japan (Hawai'i 1991; ISBN 0-8248-1353-7), IN COPYRIGHT.

THE QUOTABLE RULE TEXT. Sir James George Frazer, The Golden Bough, Chapter 22 "Tabooed Words," section 1 "Personal Names Tabooed" (3rd ed. 1911; abridged 1922; Project Gutenberg ebook 3623), PUBLIC DOMAIN: the link between a name and the person is conceived as a real and substantial bond, so that magic may be wrought on a man through his name as easily as through hair or nails. This grounds the game's name-theft mechanic in cited public-domain anthropology rather than in Earthsea (protected) or the bathhouse witch (protected).
