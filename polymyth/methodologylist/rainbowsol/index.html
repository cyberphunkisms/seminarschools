RAINBOW-AS-THRESHOLD-BETWEEN-WORLDS — PATTERN IN THE COMMONS (verified via [43] Lee & Fraser 2001 and Wikipedia 'Rainbows in mythology'): the rainbow-magic the author operates through recovers a cross-cultural pattern. Lee & Fraser's scholarly survey (U.S. Naval Academy + Penn State meteorology, 2001) found the 'bridge to the divine' concept independently across at least ten mythological systems: Zulu, Navaho (rainbow as the path of the holy spirits, depicted in sacred sandpaintings per Wikipedia), Hawaiian, Japanese (the Floating Bridge of Heaven crossed by creator deities Izanami and Izanagi — Wikipedia), Cambodian, Greek (the goddess Iris personifies the rainbow and carries messages between gods and human world — Wikipedia), Australian Aboriginal (the Rainbow Serpent as creator-being, varying by cultural tradition — Wikipedia), Chumash, Hopi, and Norse (Bifrost/Bilrost, burning rainbow bridge Midgard↔Asgard, 13th-c. Poetic Edda and Prose Edda). ADDITIONAL VERIFIED INSTANCES: Siberian Buryat shamans speak of ascending to the sky-spirit world by way of the rainbow (Wikipedia). Māori myth: Hina the moon caused a rainbow to span heavens down to earth for her mortal husband's return (Wikipedia). Philippine Tausug: seven angelic sisters Biraddali use the rainbow as a bridge to earth (Wikipedia). Slavic myth: a man touched by the rainbow is drawn to heaven, becomes a 'Planetnik' (Wikipedia). Fang of Gabon: religious initiation via 'transcendent experience when they arrive at the rainbow's center' (Wikipedia). Chinese folklore: 'Rainbow Bridge' at Miao funerals in Guizhou, soul climbs rainbow to reach moon/paradise (Pan 2007, pp. 161-165). CONTEMPORARY INSTANCES: Rainbow Bridge for pets (Edna Clyne-Rekhy, Inverness Scotland 1959, per Paul Koudounaris's 2023 Order of the Good Death research verified by National Geographic) — near-universal in Western pet-bereavement culture, explicit descent from Bifrost per Wikipedia. Nyan Cat (Christopher Torres 2011, Pop-Tart body + rainbow trail; Nyan Cat: Lost In Space game has 'parallel universes' as explicit mode; Adventure Time cameo — 'Finn and Jake end up riding a giant ginger and white cat that pukes rainbows and flies backwards' per Nyan Cat fandom wiki). Marvel Cinematic Universe Bifrost: Jane Foster identifies it as an Einstein-Rosen bridge (wormhole) in Thor (2011). Super Mario Rainbow Road (Nintendo 1996 onward, Mario Kart series): a racing track consisting of a rainbow path through space/stars, appearing in nearly every Mario Kart installment since its debut in Mario Kart 64 — not mythology proper but a contemporary rainbow-as-path-through-space instance saturating children's gaming consciousness. IMPORTANT CORRECTIONS/RETRACTIONS: Previous drafts attributed rainbow-threshold to Celtic Otherworld tradition; this was unsupported. Celtic Otherworld is reached through sidhe mounds, bodies of water, caves, and liminal times (Samhain, dawn/dusk, mist) per Wikipedia 'Celtic Otherworld' citing MacCulloch 1911, Green 1993, and the Irish Pagan School's sourcework — NOT through rainbows. The leprechaun-pot-of-gold-at-rainbow's-end is a late folk accretion (Samuel Lover 1831 is the earliest described image per Wikipedia 'Leprechaun'), not a traditional Celtic Otherworld-threshold. Leprechaun remains a legitimate cultural-commons figure at the rainbow's end but not as part of core Celtic Otherworld tradition. EXTENDED CONTEMPORARY VERIFIED INSTANCES: "Over the Rainbow" (Arlen + Harburg 1939, The Wizard of Oz; #1 song of 20th c. per NEA/RIAA 2001; per Wikipedia Dorothy's monologue positions rainbow as threshold to an inaccessible 'far, far away … behind the moon, beyond the rain') [citation 44]; Mario Kart Rainbow Road (Nintendo, Super Mario Kart SNES 1992 onward, rainbow track suspended in space as final course across entire series, most-remade track; per Super Mario Wiki) [citation 45]; Rainbow Brite (Hallmark 1983, animated 1984-86; 'Rainbow Land' traveler per Wikipedia 'Rainbow Brite'); Care Bears (American Greetings 1981, film 1985; Care-a-Lot cloud-world + Earth-travel via rainbow-and-cloud imagery per Wikipedia 'Care Bears').
