6. **The Forge canon** (Indie-RPG-design community 2001-2012). Theoretical artifacts: Lumpley Principle, Czege Principle, Big Model. **CAVEAT**: community-historical critique exists; theoretical artifacts survive on own merits.

### H.1 African TTRPG scene (T106 ABSENT — major gap filled)

**NIBCARD Games** (Kenechukwu Cornelius Ogbuagu, Nigeria, founded 2016). Nigerian Board
Card Roleplay and Dice. Won 2021 Diana Jones Award. Operates game cafe in Abuja. Convenes
annual African Boardgame Convention (AB Con); 10th anniversary in 2026. Games focus on
solving problems in society (ranching conflicts, homebuilding, Sustainable Development
Goals, migration). My World Trip created with International Organization for Migration
grant.

**Zairoo: The Pan-African Fantasy Role Playing Game** (Molombo Thillia Thillot III).
Steampunk Fantasy TTRPG with North/South/East/West/Central plus New Afrikan myths. By
African and New Afrikan creators. Kickstarter.

**Kalymba Roleplaying Game** (Craftando Games / "danipirraca" — autistic Black designer
working in TTRPG space). African-inspired tabletop RPG. Setting: legend of Aiye. Funded
via Kickstarter.

**Swordsfall** (Brandon Dixon). Afro-futurist tabletop RPG set in the world of Tikor —
inspired by pre-colonial Africa and an independent African nation with futuristic
technology. Raised $64,000+ in 24 hours on first Kickstarter (art book).

### H.2 Indian / South Asian TTRPG scene (T106 ABSENT — major gap filled, native-designer presence strong)

**Devabhumi** (Khan, Indian-origin designer, Kickstarter-funded). High fantasy TTRPG
setting inspired by history and epics of Ancient India. 5e + Pathfinder compatible.

**Maha Yodha** (2014 Kickstarter). Hindu epics card-war game. Asuras vs Adityas factions.
Combines digital art with traditional Indian artists including Magic: The Gathering
contributors.

**Against the Dark Yogi: Mythic India Roleplaying** (Tab Creations LLC). Complete
roleplaying game bringing Indian myths to the tabletop. **CAVEAT**: Western publisher;
Indian-themed not Indian-designed.

**Prani Codex — Creatures, Spirits, and Shadows from Indian Folklore for TTRPG**
(Akirang, itch.io). Bestiary from Indian folklore (Western Ghats, Himalayan, regional).

**Manthan (The Churning)** (Abhishek Kumar, itch.io). TTRPG inspired by 10th-century
Indian folktale collection Kathāsaritsāgara. Released.

**Bludgeon the TTRPG** (tacticsnchai). Tactics-focused d20 TTRPG with asynchronous class
mechanics inspired by Indian pop culture and mythology.

**[Mythic India] Abominations of Dandaranya** (rathsubhajeet, itch.io 2024). TTRPG
micro-setting based on Mythic India, compatible with Knave, Shadowdark, Old-School
Essentials.

### H.3 Polish / Eastern European TTRPG scene (T106 GAP beyond Czech/Hungarian — filled)

**Dzikie Pola / Wild Fields** (Jacek Komuda, Maciej Jurewicz, Marcin Baryłka; Wydawnictwo
MAG 1997 first edition, 2005 second edition with Komuda/Michał Mochocki/Artur Machlowski).
Polish-only language; most popular Polish RPG. 17th-century Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth
setting. Expansions: W stepie szerokim and Ogniem i mieczem.

**Wiedźmin: Gra Wyobraźni / The Witcher: Game of Imagination** (Wydawnictwo MAG, 2001).
Polish Witcher TTRPG set in Sapkowski's universe. Polish-only.

**Kryształy Czasu / Crystals of Time** (Szyndler; Wydawnictwo MAG; 1999, book edition
later). D&D-like Polish game. "Longest awaited RPG in the world" per publisher's joke.

**Oko Yrrhedesa** (Andrzej Sapkowski; Wydawnictwo MAG). Witcher-adjacent Polish.

### J.1 Russian / Ukrainian TTRPG scene (T106 GAP — partially filled)

**M87 Games** (Oleksandr Trifan, founder; Ukraine 2023-onward). Self-described first
Ukrainian TTRPG publisher. Localizing MÖRK BORG and Cairn into Ukrainian. Typography
itself is "an act of political defiance" — refusing Russian-derived font choices for the
Ukrainian edition per Trifan interview with Wargamer October 2025.

**Spirits and Monsters of Mythic Ukraine** (Oleksandr Lazorenko + Eugene Mokeiev, Kyiv).
Vaesen tabletop RPG add-on developed during the Russian invasion. Slavic-animist
folklore: kolovertii (souls of people killed or cursed; bodies buried in swamp, crossroads,
ravine, or national border). Played as resilience-building during wartime per New Lines
Magazine June-July 2023 reportage.

**Zamanora: Ballad of the Witch** (Kickstarter 2025). Slavic-folklore TTRPG compatible
with D&D 5e (2024) and Legend in the Mist. Thea-style supplement.

**Strigovia** (itch.io). Dark Slavic-inspired fantasy TTRPG setting with "debt magic"
(casting spells costs life or sanity instead of mana). Art by Przemek Truściński
(Witcher illustrator). Historical Eastern European influences. Low-fantasy.

**The City of the Steam Sun** (Gleb "Crazy Sage" Igumnov, Russia; Savage Worlds). Native-
Russian-designer TTRPG. IndieGoGo 2019.

**GURPS Russia** (S. John Ross; Steve Jackson Games 1998). 128 pages, medieval Russia
campaign supplement. **CAVEAT**: Western designer.

**Заколдованная Страна** (early Russian RPG; has text-adventure PC version).

**Эра Водолея (Age of Aquarius)** (anime occult detective Russian, WEG Star Wars
reskinned).

### J.2 Southeast Asian / #RPGSEA scene (T106 GAP — major fill, large native-designer presence)

The #RPGSEA hashtag covers TTRPG creators from Philippines, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia,
and SEA-descent diaspora. Curated database at Across RPGSEA (across-rpgsea.com).
