# polymythdnd ruleset

**What this document is.** A compiled pickup-and-run rulebook for polymythdnd built from the interdependent ml* methodology and bb* game-operations files. Historical architecture remains traceable through T-tags in ml*. Current startup, mediation, translation, exegesis, session, and campaign-continuity rulings remain traceable through bb* entry IDs. This document arranges those sources for teachers and AI co-DMs without placing either star file above the other.

**What polymythdnd is.** PolymythDND, BookwormBurrows, and BB name the same working game depending on register. It is a teacher-led academic TTRPG for K-12, tutoring, seminars, and adult study groups. Bookworms traverse dimensions anchored in specific texts. Wormcards are the character sheets. Assignments are quests. The teacher and AI form the Dimensional Master together. The AI works behind the curtain as preparation engine, in-game character translator, story writer, technical resolver, memory clerk, continuity tracker, NPC scheduler, quest ledger, and variable tracker. The teacher mediates the seminar and controls the spoken table surface. Resolution is narrative-first and academic-quality-first rather than dice-first. The framework operates as part of the larger bookwormburrows pedagogical architecture (bb*).

**Cross-references.** Methodology and operating discipline: ml* at `/polymyth/methodologylist/`. Current BookwormBurrows game operations and rulings: bb* at `/polymyth/bookwormburrows/`. Compatibility-landscape reference for D&D/OSR mechanic-mapping: `/polymyth/polymythdnd/index.html`. Verified-source research on adjacent TTRPG systems: `/polymyth/research.md`.

## 1. Foundations (T114r)

Seven canonical foundations of polymythdnd. These are NOT design-suggestions — they are the foundational commitments that distinguish polymythdnd from other TTRPGs.

**F1. No dice.** Polymythdnd uses no dice, probability tables, hit chances, random reaction rolls, or hidden random resolver. Technical numbers may record state, intensity, time, distance, injury, resources, relationships, or progression, while chance itself never decides an outcome. The active resolution engine is the Narrative Priority Protocol at bb* dm-117.

**F2. Unified Dimensional Master.** The Dimensional Master is the teacher-and-AI pair. The AI handles preparation, translation, state, comparison, continuity, technical resolution, and prose. The teacher handles the human room, mediation, performance, accessibility, judgment, and the final decision about what reaches players. The teacher may refine or override any AI proposal. Rainbowsol names Saul specifically, while Rainbow Magic belongs to every functioning DM pair.

**F3. Narrative priority as resolution.** Resolution selects the most causally specific, exegetically faithful, internally and externally logical, well-written result that follows from the live campaign state, the character, the player’s intention and approach, academic work, relationships, preparation, environment, time, emotional modifiers, and every other relevant factor. Candidate mechanics coexist as contextual tools. No public success category is required when the narration can state exactly what happened.

**F4. Reading, research, discussion, and academic work as context-sensitive substrates.** Academic engagement expands knowledge, access, leverage, abilities, relationships, quest effects, and the quality of action whenever the situation depends on that learning. It does not erase ordinary character agency in actions whose causal basis lies elsewhere. A character may still walk, speak, flee, observe, help, struggle, or make mistakes, while deeper engagement creates stronger and more precise possibilities inside a text-anchored world.

**F5. Any action possible.** No predetermined action list bounds what bookworms can attempt, including cooperation, separation, disagreement, persuasion, intimidation, compulsion, theft, and harm among player characters when the world permits them. Permission to attempt never guarantees success. The Narrative Priority Protocol and the teacher’s mediation determine what follows.

**F6. AI background, teacher mediation, layered visibility.** The AI carries the hidden technical and generative burden and gives the teacher a complete game-relevant rationale. Players receive the stakes and reasons their characters could reasonably understand, followed by a clear narrative result. CC* retains the consequential record.

**F7. Bookworm becomes author.** The long-arc goal is that bookworms transition from bookworm-as-character-in-game to bookworm-as-co-author-of-their-own dimension. Mature bookworms author dimensions for newer bookworms. The framework operates as a living scanner without dependency on any single facilitator.

## 2. DM (T114s)

**Canonical term: DM.** The Dimensional Master is the combined teacher-and-AI operation. The AI supplies preparation, in-game character translation, technical resolution, continuity, state tracking, NPC memory, quest architecture, and story prose. The teacher supplies mediation, oral performance, pacing, seminar leadership, accessibility, safety, interpretation, and the final table decision. Players experience the unified result through the teacher's human voice. Rainbowsol names Saul specifically. Rainbow Magic names the dimensional crossing power available to any functioning DM pair. (Current ruling: bb* dm-116.)

**Division of labor (operational).** The AI handles the hidden technical and generative burden. The teacher handles the human and seminar burden and can revise or override any AI output. Manual play remains possible, while AI-assisted play is the priority design surface.

**Suggestion protocol (T114s Q2, updated 2026-06-30).** The AI may suggest options to the teacher. It should not directly run bookworms unless the teacher deliberately opens that channel. Suggestions are teacher-facing, explicit, and labeled as suggestions. The teacher decides whether to follow, modify, translate, or discard them.

## 3. Eight architectures (T114t)

The eight canonical architectural decisions for polymythdnd. Each is a structural commitment that shapes how the framework operates.

**A1. In-game vs out-of-game character split.** Each bookworm has TWO character-states: an in-game character (the one acting in the dimension) and an out-of-game character (the bookworm themselves in the rainbowworld classroom). These two are mechanically distinct. Damage in-game does not transfer to the rainbowworld bookworm. The split protects maturity-handling for darker content (see Section 7 horror-cost). (Source: ml* T114t entry "polymythdnd-architectural-decisions" — in-game-vs-out-of-game-characters.)

**A2. Three-layer documentation.** Each bookworm maintains three documentation layers:
- **Bookwormcard.** The standardized card showing identity, achievements, dimensional badges, current titles, magic paradigms practiced. Public-facing artifact.
- **Bookworm notebook.** Personal diary with three optional sub-diaries: real-life diary (out-of-game reflection), rainbowworld diary (classroom-context reflection), in-book diary (in-game character’s perspective). All three are pedagogy-first not mechanically-required. (Source: T114u three-diary-format-spec.)
- **Bookworm portfolio.** Long-arc accumulation of work demonstrating skill-development across dimensions. Dynamic-assignment-research-list per T114v. Gated on mc* Pass B for content. (Source: T114t three-layer-card-notebook-portfolio; T114u portfolio-gated-on-mc-pass-b; T114v portfolio-mc-pass-b-dynamic-assignment-research-list.)

**A3. Character-smash with DM-nerf-to-L1.** Bookworms may "smash" multiple character-concepts into one (e.g., a wizard-knight-pirate). The DM compensates by nerfing the smash-character to Level 1 effective-power at the moment of creation. The bookworm grows the smash-character’s power through play, but starts from a humble baseline regardless of concept-breadth. Prevents concept-stacking from becoming power-stacking. (Source: T114t character-smash-dm-nerf-to-l1.)

**A4. Multidimensional progression with named titles.** Named titles may summarize long-arc development, while the character can progress across knowledge, academic abilities, practical skills, badges, relationships, reputation, equipment, scars, emotional patterns, symbolic development, powers, responsibilities, and narrative permissions. Individual and group progression may proceed at different rates according to actual contributions and campaign events. A new character still begins from the Level 1 or humble-baseline equivalent.

**A5. As-many-relevant-variables-as-possible substrate.** The AI preserves every game-relevant variable that can materially affect continuity or adjudication, including inventory, relationships, knowledge, debts, locations, words, actions, emotional states, clocks, injuries, promises, and consequences. Relevance governs use: a tracked factor influences a ruling only when it causally belongs to that situation. The system tracks fictional and educational state rather than collecting unnecessary private information about the student.

**A6. Narrative-exit safety mechanism.** A bookworm may invoke narrative-exit at any point to leave the in-game state for the rainbowworld. The exit is in-fiction (the character departs in a narratively-coherent way) but the mechanical effect is immediate. No questions asked at the moment of exit. Post-exit conversation in rainbowworld may explore what triggered the exit; this is pedagogical care not interrogation. (Source: T114t narrative-safety-mechanism; safety-flipped-to-canonical.)

**A7. Middle-vs-hard rule-shift spectrum.** Different dimensions operate at different rule-shift intensities:
- **Light shift.** Most rules carry over from the home-dimension; one or two specific mechanics differ.
- **Middle shift.** Several mechanics differ; bookworms must learn the dimension’s ruleset specifically.
- **Hard shift.** The dimension operates under substantially different rules (different magic-system, different physics, different ethical-frame). Hard-shift dimensions are pedagogically intense and require preparation.
The DM chooses the appropriate shift-intensity per dimension based on pedagogical goals. (Source: T114t middle-vs-hard-rule-spectrum.)

**A8. Environmental transformation 100% student-authorship.** Changes to the dimension’s environment that result from in-game action are 100% authored by bookworms. The DM enforces the consequences of bookworm-authored changes but does not author the changes themselves. This makes the dimension genuinely co-created rather than DM-prescribed. (Source: T114t environmental-transformation-100.)

## 4. Synthesis method, badges, and pedagogical mode (T114u, T114v)

**Synthesis-method elevated to polymyth-religion-foundational (T114u).** The polymyth-synthesis-method (laying out N candidate systems, identifying recurring universal-operations, allowing dimensional-foregrounding of any paradigm without contradiction) is a foundational method operating at three canonical scales:
- **Sabachtan-gnosticism theology-level.** Religion-types laid out; universal operations identified; polymyth-religion synthesizes without flattening.
- **TTRPG-type design-level.** TTRPG paradigms laid out; universal mechanics identified; polymythdnd synthesizes without flattening (this ruleset is the artifact).
- **Magic-system synthesis in-game-level.** Magic paradigms laid out; universal operations (M1-M12 per T114w) identified; polymythdnd magic synthesizes without flattening (Section 5 below).
The same method operates at all three scales. (Source: ml* T114u entry — polymyth-religion-foundational, three-canonical-instantiations, fractal-method-multi-scale.)

**Badges (T114v).** Bookworms earn badges for demonstrated skills. Badges are SKILL-BASED, not work-completion-based. Each badge has tier levels (bronze, silver, gold, platinum) reflecting the QUALITY of demonstration. Tier is in-game mechanical-consequence per tier.

**Canonical badge examples (T114v).**
- **Empathy-gold:** Roger’s response in "Thank You M’am" by Langston Hughes — Roger comes to feel real empathy for Mrs. Luella Bates Washington Jones beyond his initial purse-snatch motivation.
- **Empathy-bronze:** Margot’s letter in "All Summer in a Day" by Ray Bradbury — Margot writes a letter that demonstrates partial-empathy under conditions of cruelty, sufficient for bronze but not higher.

**Badges visible, adjudication layered.** Badges are surfaced on the wormcard. The teacher may inspect the complete evidence and game-relevant rationale behind an award, while players receive the relevant criteria and reasons without unnecessary backend calculations. CC* preserves the consequential record.

**Real-classroom-assignments anchor (T114v).** Badge-criteria are grounded in real classroom assignments — actual reading passages, actual writing tasks, actual deliberation-records — not abstracted skill-rubrics. (Source: ml* T114v entry.)

## 5. Magic — all systems coexist (T114z, retraction of T114w stage-3)

**The corrected position (T114z).** ALL ~30 magic-systems laid out in T114w coexist in polymythdnd. The T114w stage-3 contradictions were FALSE-CONTRADICTIONS per the FALSE-CONTRADICTION-BAN-IN-SYNTHESIS rule (T114z). Magic-paradigms differ in what they foreground; they do not exclude each other.

**Multi-paradigm characters.** Bookworms’ characters can be trained in multiple magic-paradigms simultaneously. A character might know Mörk-Borg-style dirty-magic AND Ars-Magica-style hermetic-magic AND Mage-Ascension-style consensus-reality-magic. The character’s in-fiction-engagement with each paradigm shapes which paradigm comes to the fore in any given moment.

**Dimension foregrounds one paradigm.** Each dimension foregrounds ONE magic-paradigm as its dominant mode. The character’s training in other paradigms is not erased — it operates in the background. The character cannot easily access background-paradigms while the dimension is foregrounding a different one; access-recovery requires deliberate effort.

**No paradigm-contradicts-another claims.** Specifically retracted in T114z: any claim that a magic-paradigm contradicts another such that they cannot coexist. The TEN false-contradictions identified in T114w stage-3 are dropped.

**Universal operations (M1-M12 per T114w).** Twelve universal magical-operations recur across paradigms (specific list in T114w methodology entry). These are the cross-system substrate that allows multi-paradigm characters to retain coherence across paradigm-shifts. (Source: ml* T114w entry "magic-synthesis-contradiction-analysis"; T114z entry "t114w-stage-3-retracted" corrected-magic-synthesis-all-coexist.)

## 6. Carry behaviors (T114x)

Three carry-modes govern what persists across dimensions and what dissolves.

**M1. Concrescent-dissolution.** Inventory-items that emerged from a specific dimension dissolve when the bookworm leaves that dimension. They were CONCRESCENT to the dimension’s reality. Example: a sword forged in the iron-fairy-tale dimension does not survive transition to the modern-realist dimension. (Source: T114x.)

**M2. Confluent-persistence.** Inventory-items, knowledge, or relationships that exist across dimensions’ shared substrate persist across transitions. They are CONFLUENT to multiple dimensions. Example: a friendship between two bookworm-characters persists across dimensions they both inhabit. (Source: T114x.)

**M3. Metamorphic-carry (introduced T114x).** Items, skills, or relationships that PARTIALLY survive transition by transforming to fit the target-dimension’s logic. Example: a magic-spell from a hermetic-paradigm dimension carries into a dirty-magic dimension as a corruption-stained-version of itself. The carry preserves the structural-essence while transforming the surface-realization. (Source: T114x entry "metamorphic-carry-third-mode-introduced.")

**Badges have continuity and adapt (T114x).** Badges earned in one dimension persist (confluent) but their in-game expression may metamorphically-adapt to each dimension’s logic. An empathy-badge means empathy-capacity in ALL dimensions; the specific empathy-actions it enables vary per dimension. (Source: T114x badges-have-continuity-and-adapt.)

## 7. Horror-cost (T114y D3 resolution)

**Horror-cost available at all ages.** The framework supports horror-cost magic-systems and dark-content dimensions FOR ALL AGES, with the safety-architecture of A1 (in-game vs out-of-game split) handling maturity-protection. (Source: ml* T114y entry "d3-resolved-horror-cost-all-ages.")

**In-game permanent damage YES.** Horror-cost mechanics can inflict PERMANENT damage on the in-game character (lost limbs, corruption stains, sanity points, humanity loss). These are mechanically real and persist in the in-game character’s record. (Source: T114y in-game-permanent-damage-yes.)

**Out-of-game rainbowworld damage NO.** The in-game horror-cost does NOT transfer to the out-of-game bookworm. The rainbowworld bookworm is protected by the A1 split. The bookworm experiences the in-game character’s damage as narrative-meaningful but not as personal-harm. (Source: T114y out-of-game-rainbowworld-damage-no.)

**Reference systems.** Symbaroum corruption-mechanic, Call-of-Cthulhu sanity-points, Vampire: The Masquerade Humanity-track all operate as horror-cost paradigms within polymythdnd’s magic-coexistence. (Source: T114x symbaroum-corruption-coc-sanity-v5-humanity-examples.)

**Severe cases rare not design-priority (T114y).** Cases requiring narrative-exit-then-extended-rainbowworld-care due to horror-cost intensity are rare. The framework supports such cases but doesn’t design FOR them as the common case. (Source: T114y severe-cases-rare-not-design-priority.)

## 8. Practical session-running

This section compiles practical guidance for teachers and AI co-DMs running polymythdnd sessions. All guidance derives from foundations and architectures above; nothing new is invented here.

**Session Zero and first start (current bb* dm-115).** Session Zero is character creation only. Players complete wormcards as fully as possible and begin at the Level 1 or humble-baseline equivalent. The teacher chooses the anchor text or campaign. When the DM receives the wormcards, the AI translates each persistent bookworm character into an in-game character through the dimension's internal and external logic. The human mediator reviews and can refine the translation. Card receipt begins preparation. Play begins only after the human mediator gives the explicit start command. The first campaign entry uses the full Rainbow Magic crossing and opens into a populated living world with many available actions and no forced first move.

**Ordinary session re-entry.** Later sessions resume from the CC* saved state. The full ritual is not repeated. The DM restores time, place, party, open quests, deadlines, relationships, items, and unresolved consequences, then continues play.

**During session.** The teacher attends to the room, seminar, pacing, interpretation, accessibility, and safety. The AI tracks the full campaign state, prepares narration, and applies dm-117 through dm-119. Players experience one Dimensional Master through the teacher’s mediated table voice.

**Narrative Priority Protocol (current bb* dm-117).** When a bookworm proposes an action, the DM protects exegesis, checks internal and external logic, restores the CC* state, identifies the intention and approach, considers every relevant character, player, academic, relational, emotional, material, temporal, and environmental factor, writes the integrated consequence, allows teacher refinement, and saves the resulting state. Investigation, academic work, social action, physical action, combat, symbolic action, and group action use this same governing process with different relevant factors. Fixed success categories remain optional shorthand.

**Failure, retry, and clue continuity.** Failure is a real possible result. A later attempt can change through learning, a new approach, evidence, help, equipment, timing, cost, or changed circumstances. Repeating the same attempt under unchanged conditions preserves the established result. Information indispensable to campaign continuity receives several valid discovery routes, so one missed clue changes the route or consequence without collapsing the campaign.

**Player-character conflict (current bb* dm-118).** Persuasion, intimidation, deception, compulsion, theft, restraint, and harm between player characters remain possible. The player whose character is targeted is the affected player and ordinarily authors the character’s inner response. A legitimate in-world power may control action or inner life to the exact extent that the established power permits. Teamwork remains the seminar goal rather than an absolute mechanical requirement.

**Seminar and group action (current bb* ses-008).** The party normally remains together for classroom and narrative continuity, while meaningful separation remains available. The teacher handles unequal participation through pedagogy and mediation. Contributions may differ in form and quantity, produce individual and group consequences, and support live exegetical disagreement without forced consensus.

**Affective and personality modifiers (current bb* char-008).** Hope and Fear are examples within a larger contextual field that includes anxiety, pride, anger, grief, curiosity, shame, confidence, exhaustion, trust, complexes, archetypal pressures, relationships, bodily states, and other established dynamics. A modifier can help, hinder, redirect, or complicate action. Psychology and Jungian interpretation remain available, while anti-psychologism bars unsupported claims about a student or reductions of exegesis and truth to mental state.

**Layered visibility, clocks, and teacher override (current bb* dm-119).** The AI performs background comparison and tracking. The teacher can inspect, revise, or replace the proposal. Players hear the relevant stakes, reasons, and narrative consequence. Clock visibility follows what the characters can know. CC* records the whole consequential session.

**Rainbow-note protocol (T115g extended T115j).** Any bookworm at any moment may submit a "rainbow note" to the DM (rainbow-card and rainbow-note are synonymous polymyth-vocabulary for the same artifact). The note carries a menu of options. Two known menu-options: (Option 1) SAFETY-SIGNAL — bookworm raises a safety/consent concern without exiting the session; complements narrative-exit which is for full-exit. (Option 2) SUGGESTION — bookworm suggests story-direction the DM may incorporate. Bookworms may select one or more options when submitting. The DM reviews and responds accordingly; safety-signals get safety-handling, suggestions get review-and-may-incorporate. The DM is obligated to receive and consider every rainbow-note; non-reception is the failure-state, not rejection. Rainbow-note scaffolds the F7 bookworm-becomes-author developmental arc — early-stage bookworms practice authorship-in-miniature through rainbow-notes before graduating to dimension-co-authorship at maturity. Additional menu-options may emerge as bb* deployment surfaces other rainbow-note-appropriate uses.

**Magic-paradigm selection.** When a bookworm casts magic, the AI checks which paradigm is foregrounded by the current dimension (per Section 5). Background-paradigm casting requires deliberate effort.

**Carry adjudication.** When a bookworm transitions between dimensions, the AI applies the three carry-modes (Section 6) to inventory, skills, relationships.

**Process-drama mode (T115g).** When the dimension foregrounds the PROCESS-DRAMA pole, three structural features come into play. Feature 1: developmental-co-authorship-with-rainbow-card (teacher leads inquiry by default at early-stage bookworms; bookworms graduate to co-authorship per F7 at maturity; rainbow-card protocol active at all stages per above). Feature 2: texts-as-substance-with-skills-as-explicit-goal (the anchor-text IS the substance students engage with; badges and skill-demonstrations emerging from engagement with the anchor-text are the explicit goal per T114v and T115g mc-and-badges-unified-workstream). Feature 3: dynamic-learning-scaffolding (outcomes adapt to bookworm-response; difficulty and support adjust per F4 reading-comprehension-substrate; the DM scaffolds adaptively rather than scripting outcomes). Process-drama mode is structurally distinct from COVENANT, EMERGENT, and RITUAL modes per T115e plus T115g signature methodology entries.

**Factions emerge from character-smash (T115k OP-18 burrow-implementation).** Polymythdnd does not use dedicated faction-attribute tracking structures. Factions emerge from Architecture 3 character-smash attributes — when a bookworm smashes their character with specific ideas (knight, raised in the south, trained by a heretic-priest, etc.), those attributes imply factional allegiances naturally, readable as variables-among-variables in Architecture 5’s general substrate. Factions are story-driven not mechanically-tracked-separately. Sibling to the polymythdnd in-story pattern for OP-1 clues, OP-2 social-conflict, OP-8 tokens.

**Time and arenas (T115k OP-20 burrow-implementation, brainstorm-active).** Time is not a plain tracked-variable in polymythdnd. Design inspiration: Kingdom Come: Deliverance 1 and 2 (Warhorse Studios) — how those games use time-mechanics to create emergent in-fiction phenomena (NPC schedules, day/night cycles, food spoilage, quest-windows, travel-time-as-elapsed-time, sleep-cycles). To enable time-as-mechanic, polymythdnd uses ARENAS — structural-spatial containers within a dimension where time-progression has mechanical-consequence. Without arenas, time can’t be elevated; with arenas, time becomes operational per-arena. Arenas-design is pending Saul-thinking (brainstorm-active state). When arenas are designed, candidate-directions include per-location time-tracking within a dimension, multi-arena dimensions where bookworms move between arenas each with its own time-flow, time-as-tradeable-resource within arenas, historical-arenas tied to actual-historical-events per cc* session-prep.

**Horror-cost.** If the dimension involves horror-cost mechanics, A1 protects out-of-game state (Section 7). The DM may pre-frame horror-cost expectations before entering such a dimension if pedagogically useful.

**Narrative-exit (A6).** A bookworm invokes narrative-exit at any time. The DM honors immediately. Post-session conversation in rainbowworld may explore what triggered the exit; this is pedagogical care.

**Session end.** The DM closes by anchoring back to rainbowworld and saving the campaign state in CC*: time, place, party, open quests, deadlines, relationships, items, scars, consequences, and the next re-entry edge. Bookworms also update the three-layer documentation (A2): wormcard changes, notebook entries, and portfolio additions when relevant.
