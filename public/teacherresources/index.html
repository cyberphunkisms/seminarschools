Hot Seating: interrogate Mrs. Jones or Roger in character
Dorothy Heathcote tradition / Drama Resource (David Farmer)
English Language ArtsGrades 6-10Lesson Pagedramaresource.com
One student or teacher sits in role as a character and answers questions from the class. For Thank You M’am: teacher-as-Mrs-Jones answers why she brought Roger home instead of calling the police; student-as-Roger answers what he was thinking when he tried to snatch the purse. In bookwormburrows deployment: this IS the NPC interrogation mechanic. The DM plays Mrs. Jones in the hot seat; bookworms ask questions; answers reveal character depth that feeds into the campaign’s moral landscape. Austin ISD published a peer-reviewed case study on hot seating in ELA (2020). Ontario OSSD: Oral Communication strand B1.
Conscience Alley: should Roger take the money from Mrs. Jones’s purse?
Drama Teachers UK / Neelands tradition
English Language ArtsGrades 6-10Lesson Pagedrama-teachers.com
Class forms two lines. A student walks between them as Roger at the moment Mrs. Jones leaves her purse unattended on the daybed. One side whispers reasons to take the money; the other side whispers reasons not to. The walker decides at the end. In bookwormburrows deployment: this maps onto any moral-choice beat in a session. The technique externalizes the character’s inner conflict as a physical exercise. Printable A4 resource available free. Ontario OSSD: Oral Communication B1 (speaking to persuade), Reading C3 (critical thinking about character motivation). CCEA Key Stage 3 statutory drama requirement.
Freeze Frame / Tableau: key moments from the story frozen and analyzed
Heathcote/Neelands tradition
English Language ArtsGrades 6-10Lesson Pagedramaresource.com
Students create physical still images of key story moments: the purse-snatch, Mrs. Jones holding Roger by his shirt, Roger washing his face at the sink, the moment Mrs. Jones gives Roger the money. Teacher taps individual students to voice their character’s thoughts (thought-tracking). In bookwormburrows: freeze frames are the session recap mechanism (re-entry beat at the start of each ordinary session per the session-start protocol). They make the transition from player-world to dimension-world physical.
Mantle of the Expert: students as Harlem community journalists investigating the purse-snatch
Dorothy Heathcote / Gavin Bolton (1995, Drama for Learning, Heinemann)
English Language ArtsGrades 7-10Lesson Pagemantleoftheexpert.com
Heathcote’s approach: students are cast as a team of experts with a commission from a fictional client. For Thank You M’am: students are journalists for the Amsterdam News (Harlem’s Black newspaper, operating in 1958) assigned to investigate a series of purse-snatchings in the neighborhood. Their investigation leads them toward the Roger/Mrs. Jones encounter. They must decide what to report, what to protect, and how to write about poverty and crime in their own community. In bookwormburrows: this IS the campaign’s real-world Harlem layer. Bookworms exploring the neighborhood as characters with a professional purpose. Academic source: Heathcote & Bolton 1995; O’Neill 1995 Drama Worlds (Heinemann); Selderslaghs PhD in progress at Royal Conservatoire Antwerp.
Role on the Wall: visual character mapping for Roger and Mrs. Jones
HFL Education / Heathcote tradition
English Language ArtsGrades 6-9Lesson Pagehfleducation.org
Draw an outline of the character on a large sheet. Outside the outline: what others see (actions, speech, appearance). Inside: what the character feels, thinks, fears, wants. For Roger: outside shows the attempted theft, the blue jeans, the dirty face; inside shows the absent parents, the desire for blue suede shoes, the shame. Supports hot seating by providing pre-work and captures learning for subsequent writing in role (e.g., the apology letter). Ontario OSSD: Reading C1 Knowledge about Texts.
