## CRITICAL CAVEATS FROM THE ORIGINAL APRIL 15 RESEARCH

**Seasonal timing is the biggest limiting factor.** The University of Toronto's exam period runs roughly April 7-17, and most regular seminar series conclude by mid-April. May through August sees dramatically fewer public events at all institutions.

**Departments that returned "no events" likely completed their winter programming before April 15** rather than having no programming at all. This includes U of T's WGSI, CDTS, IHPST, East Asian Studies, Near & Middle Eastern Civilizations, South Asian Studies, New College, as well as York's Philosophy, English, History, CERLAC, and Social and Political Thought.

**Munk School and JHI page 2 of their calendars** (likely covering May-June) could not be fully retrieved by search index due to JavaScript rendering.

**Centre for Ethics** showed no events in front-end but runs Ethics@Noon-ish (12:30-2:30 PM) frequently during term — emailing ethics@utoronto.ca is the best way to catch those.

**Massey College's event calendar** requires authentication, obscuring what is likely a rich slate of High Table dinners, Dialogues, and Walter Gordon lectures.

**Community colleges** (Sheridan, Humber, Centennial, George Brown, Seneca) do not typically host the type of public humanities lectures that universities do — no relevant events were found at any of them.

**Wilfrid Laurier, Trent Durham, and Ontario Tech** similarly returned no confirmed events for this period, though Laurier runs several annual series that may not yet be posted.

**Congress 2026 cancellation correction** (caught in May 8 update): The CSHPS PhilEvents listing dated May 30 - June 6, 2026 at George Brown is a stale 2025 rollover. The 2025 Congress at George Brown was the LAST in-person edition; 2026 is virtual-only. Source: https://universityaffairs.ca/opinion/cancelling-congress-wounds-humanities-research/
