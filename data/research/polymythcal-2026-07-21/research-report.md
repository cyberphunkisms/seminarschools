## Interpretation

The specific-event table is ready for final verification and import. Rows marked `already-present` should merge with the existing canonical record. Rows marked `source-calendar` belong in the source registry and adapter pipeline rather than the public event collection.

Large official calendars should be harvested directly. Toronto Public Library exposes thousands of filterable programs. Ville de Montréal publishes a daily-updated open dataset and an official calendar with more than 3,000 listings. Vaughan exposes 225 current listings. These sources require automated extraction and lifecycle reconciliation.

## Method

1. Searched official municipal, university, library, museum, cultural, festival, theatre, tourism, and civic calendars.
2. Recorded exact dates, ranges, recurrence, venues, languages, audiences, categories, and primary-source URLs where available.
3. Compared researched records with the 244 future Audit 14 events.
4. Preserved uncertain scope as qualified source-calendar records.
5. Classified source coverage against the Audit 14 registry.

## Limits

This file is a dated research snapshot. Official calendars change continuously. Social-only, newly announced, unindexed, ticketing-platform-only, and private events can remain outside any web research snapshot. Bulk source rows identify the route to comprehensive coverage.
