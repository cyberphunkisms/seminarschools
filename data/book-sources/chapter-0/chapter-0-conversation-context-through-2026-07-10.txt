A full circle is a thought that returns to where it began after travelling far enough to earn the return.

The starting point is revisited after reflection, evidence, contradiction, other perspectives, and synthesis. The end reconnects with the beginning, while the meaning has changed and deepened.

Snake eats itself too early: premature closure. The claim circles back before doing the work, producing contradiction, a slogan, or self-cancelling logic.
Bloated python: endless expansion. It collects connections, citations, and detours without returning to the original question or producing synthesis.
Full circle: sufficient expansion followed by meaningful return. Everything gathered along the path becomes part of the answer.

beginning → journey through difference and contradiction → synthesis → transformed return to the beginning

Circularity you already described as good
Full-circle modelling
Start with a goal, include the exact variables needed, follow their consequences, and return in time to answer the original question. The cannonball model excludes the butterfly unless the butterfly materially changes the chain of events.
Reflection
Thought returns to itself to examine where its assumptions and models came from. Your PDF explicitly defines the ouroboros as reflection and as the ability to steer among the variables into which we are thrown.
Productive paradox
The return exposes a real tension that generates further meaning. This is the paradox of meaningful bliss, contrasted with the nullifying chaos of contradiction.
Dialogue
Two reflective circles meet as a figure eight. Each person leaves their original position, passes through the other person’s reasoning, and returns with a changed model.
Seminar
Many figure-eight dialogues connect into one larger circle made of loops. Each dialogue performs a local return, while the seminar performs a collective return to the shared question. Year of the Snakes explicitly says the ouroboros grows from monologue into dialogue and seminar.
DENOTE and definition
A definition is a small model that circles a concept using the right variables. Definitions also rely on other definitions, creating a larger network of circles. Even standard fallacy literature recognizes that a sufficiently large circular dictionary can be informative, whereas a tiny premise-conclusion circle merely repeats itself.
The One or Long Ouroboros
Sustained dialectical thought repeatedly returns to earlier conclusions after gathering more evidence, perspectives, and contradictions. It remains alive because every return can add something.
Deep Ouroboros
A self-contained work can remain useful when a reader enters it, reopens it, and reconnects it to the larger conversation. The second-person subject prevents the closed text from becoming a self-consuming loop.
Narrative full circle
The ending returns to the beginning and reveals why the beginning mattered. The Idiomary deliberately places its beginning at the eventual end of its extended metaphor.
Polymorphous-mythological recursion
A figure returns through different characters, myths, actors, and historical contexts. Each iteration changes the meaning of the whole rather than simply repeating the original form.
Citation, history, and tradition
A seminar can extend beyond the people in the room. Later thinkers return to earlier texts, reinterpret them, and become material for another return. Your PDF describes seminar continuing through citation, history, and eventually tradition.
Collective ritual
Repetition becomes productive when each cycle joins individual experience to collective action and returns with greater balance or transformation. The Idiomary connects the longer ouroboros to collective ritual and historical wounds.
More situations that follow the same logic
The hermeneutic circle
A reader understands each part through the whole and revises their understanding of the whole through its parts. The circle produces interpretation because each return changes both.
Reflective equilibrium
General principles reshape judgments about particular cases, while difficult cases force revision of the principles. This is a full circle between rule and example.
Scientific model revision
A model predicts something, evidence tests it, and the result returns to alter the model. The circle remains useful when the model is simple enough to work and detailed enough for the required precision.
Engineering design
Design, prototype, testing, feedback, and redesign form a productive loop. NASA explicitly treats systems engineering processes as iterative and recursive and describes stakeholder involvement as a self-correcting feedback loop.
Control systems
The output returns as input so the system can correct itself. A balancing feedback loop keeps a system near its target, while a runaway reinforcing loop resembles a snake that grows uncontrollably or consumes its environment.
Learning through repeated return
A learner revisits the same concept after gaining new skills. The subject looks different because the learner has changed. Each return should deepen rather than duplicate the previous lesson.
Writing and revision
A draft reaches an ending, the ending reveals what the beginning needs, and the author returns to revise the beginning. The final work earns its opening retroactively.
Historical interpretation
Historians interpret surviving evidence, produce accounts, and those accounts later become archival evidence for new histories. Ricoeur describes historiography itself as a circle of interpretation in which historical writing becomes material for later explanation and rewriting.
Ethical judgment
A principle is applied to a real situation, the situation exposes limits in the principle, and the principle is refined before the next case.
Law and precedent
Existing rules shape decisions, new cases reveal gaps or tensions, and those decisions reshape how the rules are understood.
Democratic policy
A community identifies a problem, acts, receives consequences and public feedback, and revises the policy. The return creates accountability when the original goal remains visible.
Causal-loop analysis
An action changes the system that produced the action. This becomes especially important when a minor event activates a butterfly effect or domino effect and therefore earns inclusion as a variable.
Software recursion
A process calls itself productively when every return moves toward a defined base case. A missing base case produces the computational equivalent of a Bloated Python or self-consuming snake.
Music, rehearsal, and ritual performance
A rhythm returns, while variation, memory, and coordination make each return different. Repetition creates structure when it carries prior cycles forward.
Conversation repair
Participants return to an earlier statement after hearing how it affected another person. The return clarifies meaning, corrects the model, and preserves the second-person subject.
Archivalchemy
Old material is recovered, placed in a new relation, and returned to the current project with altered significance. The source remains recognizable while the new circle produces synthesis.

Good circularity

start → relevant difference → external encounter → changed understanding → timely return

Tiny Ouroboros

start → assumed conclusion → immediate return → contradiction → self-consumption

Bloated Python

start → endless variables and detours → loss of goal → failure to return

Predatory loop

self-consuming contradiction → expansion beyond itself → consumption of the viewer or second-person subject

Elementary blocks of meaning
1. Gesture

A gesture is the smallest embodied unit that can point, mark, answer, invite, refuse, imitate, or distinguish.

It is meaning beginning as movement before it becomes a full explanation.

Geometrically:

point → movement

Examples include pointing, nodding, drawing a mark, raising a hand, offering an object, or making a sound toward someone.

2. Ritual

A ritual forms when a gesture is repeated, recognized, shared, and given a stable place.

gesture+repetition+recognition=ritual

The gesture now carries memory and expectation. A handshake means more than two hands moving because prior repetitions accompany the present action.

Geometrically:

movement → repeatable curve

3. Narrative

A narrative connects gestures and rituals through time.

It establishes:

what came before
what happened
who acted
what changed
what the action meant
where the movement may lead
rituals across time=narrative

Narrative gives the ritual a world. Ritual gives the narrative an embodied form.

4. Ritual again

The narrative returns to action through ritual.

A story is remembered, taught, performed, commemorated, challenged, or rewritten through gestures. The narrative thereby changes the next enactment.

gesture→ritual→narrative→ritual→new gesture

So ritual appears twice because it is both:

the patterned action from which narrative grows
the embodied action into which narrative returns

That is a productive meaning circle.
