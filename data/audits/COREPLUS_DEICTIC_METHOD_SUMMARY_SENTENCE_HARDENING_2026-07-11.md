# CORE+ Deictic Method-Summary Sentence Hardening

Date: 2026-07-11
Scope: CORE slot 28 Rule A and root CHARTER
Status: applied to the active CORE+ source and all current mirrors

## User correction

The rejected sentence was:

> This comparison also helps me investigate anomalies, test assumptions and recognize differences that averages may hide.

The sentence belongs to the existing annotation-dependent sentence failure, so the correction strengthens that rule rather than creating a duplicate CORE slot.

## Defect

1. “This comparison” turns the method in the previous sentence into a vague abstract subject.
2. “Also helps me” announces a competence instead of advancing the analysis.
3. The three-verb list reads like a resume bullet converted into prose.
4. The sentence repeats the previous method and adds no concrete question, finding or consequence.
5. The sentence depends on the previous sentence for its actor, evidence and purpose, which interrupts paragraph flow.

## Active reversal

Fold the useful point into the sentence that performs the method. Name the concrete issue being tested.

Preferred:

> I compare the numbers with written answers, conversations and direct observation so we can understand what produced a pattern, how participants experienced it, what needs to change and whether an average is hiding important differences.

Blocked template:

> This comparison/process/approach/work also helps me [verb], [verb] and [verb].

## Composition test

Delete or rewrite the sentence when all three are true:

1. It begins by pointing backward with “This comparison,” “This process,” “This approach,” or a similar summary noun.
2. It describes what the method “also helps me” do.
3. It ends in a generic list of analytical virtues instead of a concrete question, finding or action.
