# CORE+ Annotation-Dependent Sentence Sharpening

Date: 2026-07-11
Scope: CORE slot 28 mirror, Rule A, writing mode
Status: deployed to the canonical methodologylist source

## User correction

The rejected sentence was:

> Particular attention goes to anomalies, hidden assumptions and differences that averages can conceal.

The defect extends beyond passive voice. The sentence behaves like an annotation on the previous sentence. It borrows its actor, method and purpose from prior context, pauses the paragraph to add a detached list and fails to perform the next step in the argument. This pattern produces robotic prose and disrupts paragraph flow.

## Active rule

CORE+ Rule A now names **ANNOTATION-DEPENDENT SENTENCE / PARAGRAPH-FLOW BREAK** as a prohibited writing pattern.

A sentence must remain connected to the paragraph while carrying enough of its own actor, action and purpose to advance the thought. A sentence that merely comments on the preceding sentence, adds detached emphasis or converts a bullet into prose must be removed or integrated into the active method already being described.

## Composition test

Before retaining a sentence, ask:

1. Which actor performs the action?
2. Which new step does the sentence add?
3. Does the sentence carry the paragraph forward?
4. Would the sentence become vague if the previous sentence disappeared?
5. Can the point be integrated into the method or action already underway?

## Example reversal

Rejected:

> Particular attention goes to anomalies, hidden assumptions and differences that averages can conceal.

Preferred:

> I use written responses and direct observation to identify differences that averages can conceal.
