Entry the Second

On the Man Who Is Right Even When He Is Wrong, in Chicago

The twenty-sixth of May, in the year 2026

Verachte nur Vernunft und Wissenschaft,
Des Menschen allerhöchste Kraft … (Do but despise reason and knowledge, the highest of all the powers of man …) Goethe, Faust, Part One1

You will think it perverse that I keep, in the same book as a murdered child, the ledger of a man whom nothing in this world contrives to murder, and I ask only that you read until the perversity has explained itself. The animal whose feeding I set before you keeps one customary operation against a mouth it would rather not hear, a quiet sealing-off, the petition and the disinvitation and the borrowed word stooge passed from hand to hand, and it brought that operation to bear upon a professor in Chicago with the whole weight of his own profession behind it, and the operation did not take.2 He is right when he is judged sane and right when he is called mad, and, by an arrangement I mean to lay open before you, he is right even on the occasions when he is wrong. I do not admire him, for admiration is not among the instruments issued to me. I keep an honest book, and the entry will not come to rest on the side of the cancelled, and I will show you the mechanism by which it refuses.

Set the man down first. For two long decades he has written the coldest account your century keeps of why the great powers collide, the argument that no statesman’s virtue and no nation’s wickedness governs the collision, that the structure governs it, that a great power ringed by the uncertain intentions of other great powers will reach for advantage out of fear and not out of malice, and will go on reaching until something stronger than fear restrains it.3 Then, with a colleague, he wrote the harder book still, the one that asks whether a state may be said to reason at all, and answers that it largely does, and defines the reasoning with a care your slogans cannot survive.

Hear the definition, for the definition is the whole of the engine. To reason, in that book, is not to succeed. It is to hold a credible theory of how the world works and to arrive at the decision through a true deliberation among people who disagree, and a state may reason with perfect soundness and be ruined for it, since, in the book’s own words, rational actors often fail because of factors they cannot control.4 And the book keeps a separate and named room for the other case, for the conduct that is not reasoning at all, the impulse and the dogma and the wash of feeling, and it calls that room nonrational and shuts the door upon it. So the engine stands assembled before a single war is fought, its baseline set at sanity and its deviations named in advance as the madnesses. And the man who holds such an engine cannot be unseated by a catastrophe, for the engine keeps a drawer for catastrophes, and that is the first scandal of him.

What the engine grants him is a particular sight, and the sight is the second scandal. He wears the same ground lens I lent you in the first entry, and through it the great powers are neither heroes nor monsters but a single mechanism turned upon its own reflection.5 Where your newspapers see a villain and a victim, he sees the oldest figure in the study of states, the dilemma in which each power’s reach for safety is read by the next as a threat and answered in kind, so that the move and the counter-move pursue one another with no author at the head of the pursuit.6 He sees the long discipline of answering blow with a like blow, the only discipline that has ever taught two powers to leave one another in peace.7 And behind the culture that instructs you which power to love and which to dread, he puts the one question the culture is built to forbid, the question of who paid for the song, since a great power that has learned to feed through the ear pays handsomely for its music.8

His own students have cornered him, and I will give their objections the full strength their authors could not, and answer each in its turn. One of them wrote, of the war in the east, “this was putins mistake.”9 Make the objection iron first. If the invasion was one man’s blunder, the whole structural account collapses, for a structure does not blunder, and the realist who traced the war to the slow eastward pressure of the Western alliance stands refuted by a single incompetent in a single room. The answer is that the realist never claimed the execution was competent. He claimed the provocation was real and the response predictable in kind, and a botched execution of a predictable response is the very deviation the engine names and not the counterexample that breaks it. Nor does the record collapse into one man’s error, for the war’s aims split three ways once you read it, the maximal design that would swallow the country whole, the limited design that would hold a buffer and a vow of neutrality, and the contested middle the documents decline to settle, and the size of the invading force and the alleged plan to seat a chosen man in the capital and the essay the realist published a full decade before all sit inside that unsettled middle and not inside the verdict.1011

A second wrote that the man is “blaming NATO and Ukraine for everything,” and that “that tells you all.”12 Iron it. A theory that lodges the fault forever in the same quarter is not an analysis but a reflex, and a reflex has earned the contempt the comment pays it. The answer is that to locate a cause is not to assign the whole of the blame, and the realist assigns the agency to the great power and reads the small nation as caught in a mechanism larger than its choosing, which is a harsh sentence to be handed and not at all the same as being blamed. And the verdict that it tells you all is itself the operation this diary exists to expose, for it shuts the file without reading it, which is the animal’s own gesture performed by a man who is certain he is resisting the animal. A third asked, plainly and well, “cant we solve this emirically?”13 It is the honest demand, and I will honour it by agreeing. We can, and the evidence is the very ground on which the aims split three ways, and the realist’s own method is nothing but the disciplined weighing of that evidence, so that the call for evidence is already answered and the verdict-phrase is the only party in the room that forbids the weighing.

Now stand back and read the shape of it. Twice in living memory the world has held up an atrocity as his refutation, the ruinous war in the east and the slaughter in Gaza, and twice the engine has filed the atrocity in the drawer it built in advance, for the rational course in each case was the course not taken, and the deviation confirms the baseline from which it departs.14 This is the arrangement I promised to lay open, the man who is right even when he is wrong, and you must see plainly what it reveals before you weigh what it costs. It reveals that your age has made sanity the deviant seat and the deviation the throne, so that the clear-eyed accounting of power reads, in the common light, as the madness, and the wash of feeling reads as the sense.15 The structure of it is older than the man and older than the study of states, the command already issued before the subject arrives to obey it, the conclusion always and already in its place before the evidence is ever called.16

I will not leave you with the engine and no door, for the engine has a door, and an honest devil shows it. The objection that dissolves the comfort is this one. A claim confirmed by the sane and the mad alike, by the victory and the catastrophe alike, has begun to forbid the very evidence that might unseat it, and an account no possible event can refute has crossed, without announcing the crossing, from a description of how states behave into a doctrine that cannot be wrong.17 The move that defines reasoning as the soundness of the process and not the success of the outcome is the precise hinge upon which the crossing turns, for it withdraws the one test a watching world might apply, which is whether the reasoning worked. Ring the man with a protective belt of auxiliary excuses and you have saved the theory at the price of its science. And the man himself has not always held the line between the cold description of power and the warm prescription of a policy, as his most quarrelled-over book makes plain.

Plainly. The realist may be a seer who reads the machine the rest of you cannot, or he may be the keeper of a box that no fact is permitted to open, and from inside the machine the two cannot be told apart. That is the one line in this entry that will not balance.

Weigh, before you decide, what it costs to seal off the man for the worse of his two heresies. To silence him for naming the slaughter a genocide, you must first deny that it is one, and to deny that it is one you must set aside the finding of the highest court that the charge is at the least plausible under the very convention a survivor of the last great slaughter wrote into the law.18 So the sealing-off cannot be performed without enacting the precise unreason the engine foretold, and the crowd that gathers to silence the clear-eyed man must blind itself in the gathering. The animal does not mind. It has fed on stranger meals.

Guard yourself, last of all, against the sweetest error, the belief that to see truly is to be good. It is not, and the diary would be a liar to pretend it. There is a credentialed seer in another field entirely, a man who took the academy’s highest degree for the study of the mind’s far edges and was disbelieved for the whole of his life for what he carried back from them.19 To be right, or to be thought right by those who matter, is a species of power, and power is not virtue. The clearest proof of it is the comedian your decade first crowned and then disgraced, whose craft stayed exact while the man behind the craft did a squalid harm, who was right about how a joke is built on the very evenings he was wrong about nearly all that matters.20 To wear the lens is to come into a power, and what is done with the power the lens does not decide, and the man who reads the machine without a flaw may yet be a wretch, and the diary records the sight and withholds the halo.

Here is the turn the animal would forbid. The one who asked aloud who paid for the song is the one the paid-for song now labours to silence, and the debt, you will notice, runs in the unexpected direction, from the culture to the man it cannot answer and so must banish.21 The sight he holds is not a gift he was born holding. It is a lens ground slowly, by a discipline a patient person may take up, and the one who grinds a lens ceases to relay the song and begins to keep a clean account of his own, an inscriber of his own rite in place of a host of the animal’s.22 You have now watched two entries through that lens, the child the animal ate and the man it cannot eat, and the animal would have you set the lens down and forget the grinding of it, and I would not.

The one line that will not balance is whether the man sees or whether his box is merely shut, and that line I leave standing open, for it is the honest place to leave it, and the honest place is the only ground on which this book is kept.

Entry the second. Closed, but for that one line, and for the reader who now holds the lens.
