# Upcoming Academic Lectures and Events in Philosophy, Humanities, and Social Sciences: Toronto, GTA, and Surrounding Region

**From April 15 through fall 2026, at least 70+ confirmed or announced public academic events span the Toronto/GTA region**, hosted by the University of Toronto (across all three campuses and its federated colleges), York University, McMaster, the University of Guelph, Toronto Metropolitan University, OCAD University, and community institutions like the Toronto Public Library and Royal Ontario Museum. The densest period runs mid-April through late May, after which summer schedules thin considerably. Many departments had not yet posted full spring/summer schedules as of April 14, 2026 — checking the resource URLs listed at the end is strongly recommended.

The table below is sorted chronologically. Events with unconfirmed dates ("TBD Spring 2026") are grouped at the end. All information was gathered directly from institutional event calendars, PhilEvents, and departmental websites on April 14, 2026.

## Important caveats about coverage

**Seasonal timing is the biggest limiting factor.** The University of Toronto's exam period runs roughly April 7–17, and most regular seminar series (philosophy colloquia, reading groups, brown bags) conclude by mid-April. May through August sees dramatically fewer public events at all institutions. Many of the departments that returned "no events" — including U of T's WGSI, CDTS, IHPST, East Asian Studies, Near & Middle Eastern Civilizations, South Asian Studies, and New College, as well as York's Philosophy, English, History, CERLAC, and Social and Political Thought — likely completed their winter programming before April 15 rather than having no programming at all.

**Munk School has additional events on page 2 of its calendar** (likely covering May–June) that could not be fully retrieved. The same is true for JHI's page 2. The Centre for Ethics showed no events but runs the Ethics@Noon series (12:30–2:30 PM) frequently during term — emailing ethics@utoronto.ca is the best way to catch those. Massey College's event calendar requires authentication, obscuring what is likely a rich slate of High Table dinners, dialogues, and Walter Gordon lectures. **Community colleges** (Sheridan, Humber, Centennial, George Brown, Seneca) do not typically host the type of public humanities lectures that universities do — no relevant events were found at any of them. **Wilfrid Laurier, Trent Durham, and Ontario Tech** similarly returned no confirmed events for this period, though Laurier runs several annual series that may not yet be posted.

The highest-density opportunities for attending humanities events in the coming weeks center on **U of T's St. George campus** (particularly the Jackman Humanities Building at 170 St. George and the Munk School), with the week of April 21–25 standing out as especially rich: the Spinoza conference, Ancient Philosophy Workshop, Havel discussion, Emma Dench lecture, Latin poetry workshop, ASA methodology meeting, and the Avie Bennett literature lecture all fall within a five-day window.
