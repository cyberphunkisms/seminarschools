# Sentai terminology correction — 25 June 2026

## Decision

The archive’s earlier split team labels have been retired. Their underlying design functions remain fully active under clear, stable terms.

## What remains canon

- **Sentai**: the whole learning-and-play collective around a campaign: Rainbowsol, Bookworms, returning Bookworms who serve as guides, and any educator present. The Sentai exists across the threshold.
- **Party**: the Bookworms who cross into a particular dimension in a particular session. They carry their Bookwormcards into that world as its player-characters.
- **Guides**: returning Bookworms and educators who support the party without taking its choices away.
- **Rainbowsol**: the threshold figure who gathers the Sentai, conducts the burrow ritual, and guides the party through the portal.

## Preserved design functions

This correction preserves the three distinct functions carried by the earlier vocabulary:

1. a cross-session collective of learners and mentors;
2. a particular in-world party with agency over its own choices;
3. a threshold role that carries the party across and holds the Dimensional Master register.

## Archive changes

- Rewrote the narrative-architecture team definition as **Sentai and party**.
- Updated age-band, project, mirror-crossing, ghost-exegesis, and session-structure references so they name the relevant Bookworm, party, guide, or Sentai.
- Updated source pages, generated pages, and the search concordance.
- Preserved campaign mechanics, quests, assignments, history, and continuity architecture.
