# Saul CV Website and PDF — All-Perspective Critique and Final Revision

## Governing decision

The website and the downloadable CVs serve different visual purposes.

- **Website:** visual-first, personal, mildly spectral, geometric, exploratory and modular.
- **Designed PDF:** professional, restrained, one-page, print-safe and role-facing.
- **ATS PDF:** single-column, text-first and machine-readable.
- **Plain text:** portable application copy.

The visual website remains the source experience. The PDFs derive from the selected role focus without importing the website's rainbow or geometry.

## What was wrong before this release

### Live `/main/` writing list

The writing grid allowed the title column to collapse until long names broke into one character per line. The colour and geometry were alive, while the text hierarchy stopped functioning. The repair preserves the live colour system and replaces the unstable title/description proportions with a resilient grid.

### Download-first Saul overhaul

The previous overhaul reversed the intended hierarchy. It foregrounded output configuration, created too many simultaneous choices, hid the portrait/map/archive behind utility patterns and made the webpage feel like a résumé generator. The old visual page survived in the files but lost its governing position.

### Older hospitality PDF

The older one-page hospitality PDF had a valuable editorial premise: a clear identity heading, strong rules, a date rail, a chronology and a compact sidebar. Its execution was too dense, used very small type, mixed hospitality with research/education material, showed historical credentials without enough status context and did not expose reliable extractable text.

### Recent role PDFs

The recent role PDFs became generic and sparse. The hierarchy looked like a default word-processing template, the page contained large unused areas, role selection included weak or repeated evidence and the visual relationship to the stronger earlier editorial folio disappeared.

## Final website revision

### Visual designer perspective

- Restored one translucent editorial card rather than a dashboard.
- Preserved the mild spectrum as a thin top rhythm and semantic focus dots.
- Kept geometry visible through the surface instead of hiding it behind opaque panels.
- Returned the portrait, name, role, profile and contact information to the opening composition.
- Preserved the map and complete dated work record in the ordinary page flow.
- Used one visual system across `/saul/`, all twelve focused CV routes and the hospitality alias.

### Information architecture perspective

- Eight common focus areas remain immediately visible.
- Four less common focus areas sit inside one compact disclosure.
- One primary action downloads the professional PDF.
- ATS, plain text and print sit inside one secondary application-formats disclosure.
- Additive combinations remain available inside one separate disclosure.
- The page avoids presenting every capability and every format at the same visual level.

### Recruiter and hiring-manager perspective

- Identity, role, profile and contact information are visible before controls.
- The page shows five role-relevant records and a concise skills column immediately.
- Direct links open the correct role focus without changing the page's visual identity.
- The complete history remains available when a reader needs depth.

### Hospitality-manager perspective

- The hospitality view foregrounds counter service, POS/cash, espresso and beverage preparation, food preparation, sanitation, FIFO/inventory, opening/closing and shift supervision.
- Montreal appears only in the earlier crew-member role.
- The later Assistant Manager role remains Toronto-based.
- Latte art is absent.
- Historical training and licensing are labelled historical.

### Applicant perspective

- The page feels like a coherent professional identity rather than a form.
- A user can share `/saul/cv/hospitality/`, `/saul/cv/teaching/` or any other focused route directly.
- The selected role has a clear professional PDF without making the applicant choose among a wall of downloads.
- Combined areas remain shareable and printable.

### Accessibility perspective

- One H1 and one main landmark govern the page.
- Focus choices are links, while combinations use native checkboxes.
- Secondary formats and advanced combinations use native `details`/`summary` controls.
- Visible focus states, reduced-motion handling, responsive rules and the site-wide type/zoom contract remain active.
- Desktop and mobile audits found no horizontal overflow.

### Mobile perspective

- The portrait, identity and role stack in a readable order.
- The focus grid collapses without character-level wrapping.
- The experience and skills columns become a single reading flow.
- Secondary controls remain collapsed until requested.

### SEO and link-sharing perspective

- Each focused route has a unique title, description, canonical URL and ProfilePage/Person structured data.
- Every focused route renders the same modular card with its matching default focus.
- The hospitality alias remains available and points to the canonical focused route.
- The sitemap and route-classification contracts remain valid.

### Performance and engineering perspective

- The page uses static HTML, one canonical JSON record and shared local CSS/JavaScript.
- One generator produces every focused route and every PDF family.
- Source and public copies are rebuilt together.
- The FINAL6 rule remains intact: no top-level `public/data`, `public/scripts` or other forbidden internal directory enters the deploy surface.

### Preservation perspective

- The mild rainbow remains website-only.
- Geometry remains present on every webpage.
- The portrait, map, timeline, practice filters and complete archive remain part of the main CV experience.
- Performance remains distinct.
- Portfolio remains distinct.
- Education controls the MA postnominal.

## Final PDF revision

### Human-facing designed PDFs

- Twelve one-page role PDFs use a fixed charcoal and cool-gray professional palette.
- No spectrum strip, role colour, rainbow, portrait or geometry appears.
- A strong serif name heading and restrained masthead restore editorial authority.
- The profile sits in a quiet summary band.
- Experience uses a date rail and readable role hierarchy.
- Skills, credentials, languages and the focused web route sit in a structured sidebar.
- Every role uses the same system, while content changes according to the selected focus.
- Long content is edited to fit rather than ending in an ellipsis.

### ATS PDFs

- Twelve one-page PDFs use a single-column reading order.
- Text extraction is verified.
- No embedded images are used.
- Contact, role, profile, skills, experience, training and languages remain selectable and searchable.

### Complete archive

- A separate four-page archive retains the complete career record.
- The long archive is distinct from role-facing one-page applications.

### Factuality and reputational perspective

- BUMI replaces the incorrect BJMI spelling.
- BUMI duties retain the supplied event-operation scope in concise professional language.
- Farsi is advanced; French and Mandarin are basic.
- AODA is dated June 2026.
- Bronze Cross/First Aid, Smart Serve, Food Handler training and the security licence carry historical context.
- Academic material appears only in Education-selected or complete-academic output.
- Availability remains outside the CV.

## Final synthesis

The website now carries the colour, geometry, portrait, movement and personal identity. The PDFs carry professional restraint, scanability, role relevance and print discipline. Direct links keep the correct role focus open without creating a different website for every route. Downloads remain excellent without becoming the reason the page exists.
